# Bent Over Row with V Bar

## Quick answer

The bent over row with V bar is a machine-based exercise targeting the upper back, with secondary emphasis on the bicep and forearms. It involves pulling a V bar attachment towards the abdomen while maintaining a bent over position, focusing on squeezing the shoulder blades together. Intermediate back exercise using machine. Targets upper back, also works biceps, forearms.

- **Body Part:** Back
- **Equipment:** Machine
- **Target Muscle:** Upper Back
- **Secondary Muscles:** Biceps, Forearms
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Adjust the seat height and position yourself facing the machine.
2. Grasp the V bar with an overhand grip, keeping your back straight and your knees slightly bent.
3. Pull the V bar towards your abdomen, squeezing your shoulder blades together.
4. Pause for a moment at the top of the movement, then slowly release the weight back to the starting position.
5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Use the V-bar attachment on a barbell or cable to maintain a neutral (palms-facing) grip, which is easier on the wrists.
- Keep your back at about a 45-degree angle and pull the V-bar toward your navel, not your chest.
- Squeeze your shoulder blades together at the top and hold for one second before lowering.

## Common mistakes

- Standing too upright, which reduces the range of motion and turns it into a shrug-like movement.
- Jerking the weight up with the lower back instead of pulling with the mid-back and lats.
- Letting the weight pull your shoulders forward at the bottom, rounding the upper back.

## FAQ

### What does the V-bar grip do differently for rows?

The neutral grip V-bar reduces wrist strain and shifts emphasis slightly toward the middle back and lower lats compared to a pronated (overhand) grip.

### Is a V-bar row better than a standard Bent Over Row?

Neither is better; they target slightly different areas. The V-bar emphasizes the mid-back and lower lats, while a wide overhand row hits the upper back and rear delts more.

### Can I use a V-bar on a standard barbell?

Yes, you can straddle the end of a loaded barbell and use a V-bar handle under it, creating a T-Bar Row setup.

### What muscles does the Bent Over Row with V Bar work?

The Bent Over Row with V Bar primarily targets the upper back, with secondary activation in the biceps, forearms. It's classified as a strength exercise for the back.

### Is the Bent Over Row with V Bar good for beginners?

The Bent Over Row with V Bar is rated as intermediate. Beginners should build a base of upper back strength with easier variations first.
