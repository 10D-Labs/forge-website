# Dumbbell Single Arm Lateral Raise with Support

## Quick answer

The dumbbell one arm lateral raise with support is an isolation exercise targeting the deltoid muscles of the shoulder. By using a support, it allows for better form and focus on the shoulder muscles, reducing the risk of using momentum or improper technique. Beginner shoulders exercise using dumbbell. Targets shoulders, also works trapezius, rotator cuff.

- **Body Part:** Shoulders
- **Equipment:** Dumbbell
- **Target Muscle:** Shoulders
- **Secondary Muscles:** Trapezius, Rotator Cuff
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a dumbbell in one hand, with your palm facing your body.
2. Place your other hand on a stable surface, such as a bench or wall, for support.
3. Keep your back straight and engage your core.
4. Raise the dumbbell out to the side, keeping your arm straight and your palm facing down.
5. Continue lifting until your arm is parallel to the ground.
6. Pause for a moment at the top, then slowly lower the dumbbell back down to the starting position.
7. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, then switch sides.

## Pro tips

- Use the support hand on a bench or wall to stabilize your torso, allowing the working delt to lift without any body lean.
- Lean your body slightly toward the support to lengthen the range of motion on the working arm.
- Raise the dumbbell to just below shoulder height with a slight elbow bend, palm facing the floor.

## Common mistakes

- Relying too much on the support hand and pushing off it to assist the raise, which defeats the purpose.
- Standing too upright instead of leaning slightly into the support, which reduces the effective range of motion.
- Shrugging the working shoulder upward, recruiting the upper trap instead of isolating the lateral delt.

## FAQ

### What is the benefit of using support during Lateral Raises?

The support eliminates balance demands and body sway, letting you isolate the lateral delt more strictly. It also allows a slight lean that increases the working range of motion.

### Can I use a wall instead of a bench for support?

Yes. Any stable surface works. A wall, squat rack, or bench will all provide adequate support.

### Is this variation better for beginners?

Yes. The support makes it easier to maintain strict form and learn the Lateral Raise movement pattern before progressing to unsupported versions.

### What muscles does the Dumbbell Single Arm Lateral Raise with Support work?

The Dumbbell Single Arm Lateral Raise with Support primarily targets the shoulders, with secondary activation in the trapezius, rotator cuff. It's classified as a strength exercise for the shoulders.

### Is the Dumbbell Single Arm Lateral Raise with Support good for beginners?

Yes, the Dumbbell Single Arm Lateral Raise with Support is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building shoulders strength.
