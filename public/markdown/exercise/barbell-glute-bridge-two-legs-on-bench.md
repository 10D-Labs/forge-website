# Barbell Glute Bridge Two Legs on Bench

## Quick answer

The barbell glute bridge with two legs on a bench is a compound lower body exercise that primarily targets the glutes, while also engaging the hamstrings and core. It is performed by resting your upper back on a bench, placing a barbell across your hips, and driving your hips upward to create a straight line from your knees to your shoulders. Intermediate legs exercise using barbell. Targets glutes, also works hamstrings, core.

- **Body Part:** Legs
- **Equipment:** Barbell
- **Target Muscle:** Glutes
- **Secondary Muscles:** Hamstrings, Core
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Start by sitting on the edge of a bench with your upper back resting against it and your feet flat on the ground, hip-width apart.
2. Place a barbell across your hips, holding it securely with both hands.
3. Engage your glutes and core muscles, then press through your heels to lift your hips off the bench, creating a straight line from your knees to your shoulders.
4. Pause for a moment at the top, squeezing your glutes.
5. Slowly lower your hips back down to the starting position.
6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Pad the barbell with a hip thrust pad or folded towel to protect your hip bones.
- Position your upper back against the bench edge at the lower part of your shoulder blades for optimal leverage.
- Drive through both heels and squeeze your glutes at the top for a 2-second hold on every rep.

## Common mistakes

- Hyperextending the lower back at the top by pushing the hips too high past the neutral spine position.
- Placing the back too high on the bench, which restricts range of motion and can strain the neck.
- Using too much weight and compensating with a shortened range of motion or lumbar extension.

## FAQ

### How is this different from a Barbell Hip Thrust?

They're essentially the same movement. The bench-supported glute bridge and hip thrust are interchangeable terms. Both use the bench for back support with a barbell across the hips.

### How much weight should I use?

Start with 95 lbs and add weight in 10-20 lb increments. Most intermediate lifters work in the 135-225 lb range. The glutes are strong muscles that handle heavy loads well.

### Can this exercise replace squats for glute training?

It's one of the best glute isolation exercises, but it doesn't train the quads, core, or movement patterns like a squat does. Use both for complete lower body development.

### What muscles does the Barbell Glute Bridge Two Legs on Bench work?

The Barbell Glute Bridge Two Legs on Bench primarily targets the glutes, with secondary activation in the hamstrings, core. It's classified as a strength exercise for the legs.

### Is the Barbell Glute Bridge Two Legs on Bench good for beginners?

The Barbell Glute Bridge Two Legs on Bench is rated as intermediate. Beginners should build a base of glutes strength with easier variations first.
