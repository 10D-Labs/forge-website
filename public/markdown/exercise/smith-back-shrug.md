# Smith Back Shrug

## Quick answer

The smith back shrug is a strength exercise targeting the trapezius muscles, performed using a smith machine. It involves lifting the shoulders straight up towards the ears while holding a barbell, focusing on squeezing the traps at the top of the movement. Beginner back exercise using smith machine. Targets traps, also works shoulders.

- **Body Part:** Back
- **Equipment:** Smith Machine
- **Target Muscle:** Traps
- **Secondary Muscles:** Shoulders
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and your knees slightly bent.
2. Grasp the barbell with an overhand grip, hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
3. Keep your arms straight and allow the barbell to hang in front of your thighs.
4. Lift your shoulders straight up towards your ears, squeezing your traps at the top.
5. Hold for a moment, then lower your shoulders back down to the starting position.
6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Stand with the bar behind you to target the mid and lower traps more than front shrugs.
- Squeeze your shoulder blades together as you shrug up to emphasize the retraction component.
- Hold the top position for 2-3 seconds for maximum trap engagement.

## Common mistakes

- Rolling the shoulders forward or backward instead of shrugging straight up and slightly back.
- Using too much weight and barely moving the shoulders, resulting in minimal trap activation.
- Bending the elbows to use the biceps to assist the shrug.

## FAQ

### What's the advantage of behind-the-back shrugs?

The bar position behind the body naturally pulls the shoulders into slight retraction, which targets the mid traps and rhomboids more than front shrugs. It's also more comfortable for some people.

### Are Smith machine shrugs better than Dumbbell Shrugs?

The Smith machine allows you to focus entirely on the trap contraction without worrying about bar path. Dumbbells allow more natural movement. Use both for complete trap development.

### How often should I train traps?

Traps can be trained 2-3 times per week since they recover relatively quickly. Include both shrug variations and rowing movements for full trap development.

### What muscles does the Smith Back Shrug work?

The Smith Back Shrug primarily targets the traps, with secondary activation in the shoulders. It's classified as a strength exercise for the back.

### Is the Smith Back Shrug good for beginners?

Yes, the Smith Back Shrug is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building traps strength.
