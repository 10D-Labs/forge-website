# Dumbbell Over Bench Reverse Wrist Curl

## Quick answer

The dumbbell over bench reverse wrist curl is an isolation exercise targeting the forearms, specifically the wrist extensors. It is performed seated with the forearms resting on a bench and the wrists hanging off the edge, curling the dumbbells upward with the backs of the hands facing up. Beginner arms exercise using dumbbell. Targets forearms, also works biceps, brachialis.

- **Body Part:** Arms
- **Equipment:** Dumbbell
- **Target Muscle:** Forearms
- **Secondary Muscles:** Biceps, Brachialis
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Sit on a bench with your feet flat on the ground and hold a dumbbell in each hand, palms facing down.
2. Rest your forearms on the bench, allowing your wrists to hang off the edge.
3. Slowly curl your wrists upward, bringing the dumbbells towards your body.
4. Pause for a moment at the top, then slowly lower the dumbbells back down to the starting position.
5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Rest both forearms on the bench with palms facing down, wrists hanging off the edge, and curl upward to target the extensors.
- Perform the movement slowly, squeezing the top of your forearm at the peak of each rep for 1 second.
- Keep the dumbbells light; the wrist extensors are small muscles that benefit from high reps and controlled tempo.

## Common mistakes

- Using heavy weight that forces you to jerk the wrists up rather than performing a smooth, controlled curl.
- Letting the forearms slide or lift off the bench during the movement, which reduces isolation.
- Only performing partial range of motion by not lowering the wrists fully before each rep.

## FAQ

### Why are reverse wrist curls important?

They strengthen the wrist extensors, which are often underdeveloped compared to the flexors. This imbalance can lead to wrist pain and reduced grip performance.

### How many reps should I do for reverse wrist curls?

15-25 reps per set works well. The wrist extensors are endurance-oriented and respond best to higher rep ranges.

### Can reverse wrist curls help prevent carpal tunnel?

Strengthening the forearm extensors can improve wrist stability and may help with prevention, but it is not a substitute for medical treatment if symptoms exist.

### What muscles does the Dumbbell Over Bench Reverse Wrist Curl work?

The Dumbbell Over Bench Reverse Wrist Curl primarily targets the forearms, with secondary activation in the biceps, brachialis. It's classified as a strength exercise for the arms.

### Is the Dumbbell Over Bench Reverse Wrist Curl good for beginners?

Yes, the Dumbbell Over Bench Reverse Wrist Curl is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building forearms strength.
