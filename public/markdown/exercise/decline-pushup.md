# Decline Pushup - Forge Exercise Library

## Quick Answer

The decline pushup is a bodyweight exercise that targets the lower pectoral muscles by performing a pushup with the hands elevated on a surface such as a bench or step. This variation reduces the load compared to a standard pushup, making it more accessible for beginners.

**Details:**
- **Body Part:** Chest
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Chest
- **Secondary Muscles:** Triceps, Shoulders
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to Perform Decline Pushup

1. Place your hands on an elevated surface, such as a bench or step, slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
2. Extend your legs behind you, resting on the balls of your feet, creating a straight line from your head to your heels.
3. Lower your chest towards the elevated surface by bending your elbows, keeping your body in a straight line.
4. Pause for a moment at the bottom, then push yourself back up to the starting position by straightening your arms.
5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
