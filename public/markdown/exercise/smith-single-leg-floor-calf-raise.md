# Smith Single Leg Floor Calf Raise

## Quick answer

The smith one leg floor calf raise is a lower leg exercise performed using a Smith machine. It targets the calves and also engages the hamstrings and glutes as secondary muscles. The exercise involves raising your heel off the ground while balancing on one leg, with the Smith machine bar providing resistance. Intermediate legs exercise using smith machine. Targets calves, also works hamstrings, glutes.

- **Body Part:** Legs
- **Equipment:** Smith Machine
- **Target Muscle:** Calves
- **Secondary Muscles:** Hamstrings, Glutes
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Position yourself on the floor under the smith machine bar, facing away from the machine.
2. Place the balls of your feet on a raised surface, such as a weight plate or block.
3. Position the smith machine bar across your lower legs, just above your ankles.
4. Hold onto the bar with your hands for stability.
5. Raise your heels off the ground by extending your ankles, lifting your body up.
6. Pause at the top of the movement, then slowly lower your heels back down to the starting position.
7. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Stand on a weight plate or block under the Smith bar for increased range of motion at the ankle.
- Position the bar across your upper traps and keep your core tight to prevent lateral swaying.
- Wrap your non-working foot behind your working ankle to keep it out of the way.

## Common mistakes

- Standing directly on the flat floor without elevation, which severely limits the calf stretch at the bottom.
- Loading too much weight before mastering balance on one leg, leading to ankle instability.
- Letting the bar shift to one side because the single-leg stance creates an uneven force.

## FAQ

### Why use the Smith machine for single-leg calf raises?

The fixed bar path provides stability that makes single-leg calf raises safer and lets you focus on the calf contraction without worrying about balancing a barbell.

### How much weight should I start with?

Start with just the bar (typically 15-20 pounds) and add weight gradually. The single-leg demand plus the Smith bar weight is enough for most beginners.

### Can I superset left and right legs?

Yes. Alternating legs with no rest between sides is an efficient way to train and keeps your heart rate elevated while each calf gets brief recovery.

### What muscles does the Smith Single Leg Floor Calf Raise work?

The Smith Single Leg Floor Calf Raise primarily targets the calves, with secondary activation in the hamstrings, glutes. It's classified as a strength exercise for the legs.

### Is the Smith Single Leg Floor Calf Raise good for beginners?

The Smith Single Leg Floor Calf Raise is rated as intermediate. Beginners should build a base of calves strength with easier variations first.
