# Standing Single Leg Curl

## Quick answer

The standing single leg curl is a bodyweight exercise targeting the hamstrings and glutes. It involves balancing on one leg while curling the opposite heel toward the glutes, emphasizing hamstring activation and single leg stability. Beginner legs exercise using body weight. Targets hamstrings, also works glutes.

- **Body Part:** Legs
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Hamstrings
- **Secondary Muscles:** Glutes
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Stand with your feet hip-width apart and your hands on your hips.
2. Shift your weight onto your left leg and lift your right foot off the ground, bending your knee.
3. Slowly curl your right heel towards your glutes, squeezing your hamstring.
4. Pause for a moment at the top, then slowly lower your right foot back down to the starting position.
5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, then switch legs.

## Pro tips

- Curl your heel slowly toward your glute, squeezing the hamstring hard at the top for 2 seconds.
- Keep your standing knee slightly bent and your hips square to the front.
- Hold onto a wall or chair for balance so you can focus entirely on the hamstring contraction.

## Common mistakes

- Swinging the leg up with momentum instead of curling with a controlled hamstring contraction.
- Tilting the pelvis or arching the lower back to compensate for tight hip flexors.
- Not achieving full range of motion by stopping the curl before the heel reaches glute height.

## FAQ

### Is the Standing Single Leg Curl effective without weight?

It's a good activation exercise and warm-up drill, but it becomes too easy quickly for building strength. Add an ankle weight or resistance band to keep it challenging.

### How do I make the standing leg curl harder?

Add an ankle weight, loop a resistance band around your ankle, or slow the tempo down to 5 seconds per rep. You can also squeeze and hold at the top for 3-5 seconds.

### Can this exercise help with hamstring strains?

It's a good rehabilitation exercise for mild hamstring strains because it allows you to control the load precisely. Start with bodyweight and pain-free range of motion, progressing gradually.

### What muscles does the Standing Single Leg Curl work?

The Standing Single Leg Curl primarily targets the hamstrings, with secondary activation in the glutes. It's classified as a strength exercise for the legs.

### Is the Standing Single Leg Curl good for beginners?

Yes, the Standing Single Leg Curl is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building hamstrings strength.
