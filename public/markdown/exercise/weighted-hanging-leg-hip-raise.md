# Weighted Hanging Leg Hip Raise

## Quick answer

The weighted hanging leg hip raise is an advanced core exercise that targets the abdominal muscles, with additional emphasis on the hip flexors and lower back. The movement requires hanging from a pullup bar while lifting weighted legs up to engage the core. Advanced core exercise using body weight. Targets abs, also works hip flexors, lower back.

- **Body Part:** Core
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Abs
- **Secondary Muscles:** Hip Flexors, Lower Back
- **Difficulty:** Advanced
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Hang from a pullup bar with your arms fully extended and your palms facing away from you.
2. Engage your core and lift your legs up in front of you, keeping them straight.
3. Continue lifting until your legs are parallel to the ground or slightly higher.
4. Pause for a moment at the top, then slowly lower your legs back down to the starting position.
5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Secure a light dumbbell between your feet or use ankle weights to add resistance progressively.
- Initiate the movement by curling your pelvis upward, not just swinging your legs.
- Use a dead hang position and engage your lats to prevent swinging before each rep.

## Common mistakes

- Swinging excessively and using momentum rather than controlled hip flexion and pelvic curling.
- Adding too much weight before mastering the bodyweight version with full Pelvic Tilt at the top.
- Gripping too tightly and fatiguing the forearms before the abs are worked sufficiently.

## FAQ

### How much weight should you add to Hanging Leg Raises?

Start with 2-5 pounds between your feet or with ankle weights. Even a small amount of weight significantly increases the difficulty due to the long lever arm.

### Is this exercise safe for your lower back?

Yes, when done correctly. The hanging position actually decompresses the spine. Just avoid excessive swinging, which can jar the lower back.

### What is the difference between a Hanging Leg Raise and a Hanging Leg Hip Raise?

The hip raise version adds a Pelvic Tilt at the top, curling the hips toward the ribcage. This recruits the lower abs far more than stopping at the hip flexion point.

### What muscles does the Weighted Hanging Leg Hip Raise work?

The Weighted Hanging Leg Hip Raise primarily targets the abs, with secondary activation in the hip flexors, lower back. It's classified as a strength exercise for the core.

### Is the Weighted Hanging Leg Hip Raise good for beginners?

The Weighted Hanging Leg Hip Raise is an advanced exercise. Beginners should master fundamental core movements first.
