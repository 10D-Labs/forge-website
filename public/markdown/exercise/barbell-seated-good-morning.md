# Barbell Seated Good Morning

## Quick answer

The barbell seated good morning is a strength exercise that targets the glutes, with secondary emphasis on the hamstrings and lower back. It involves sitting on a bench with a barbell on your upper back, hinging forward at the hips, and returning to the upright position. Intermediate legs exercise using barbell. Targets glutes, also works hamstrings, lower back.

- **Body Part:** Legs
- **Equipment:** Barbell
- **Target Muscle:** Glutes
- **Secondary Muscles:** Hamstrings, Lower Back
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Sit on a bench with your feet flat on the ground and a barbell resting on your upper back.
2. Keep your back straight and your chest up.
3. Slowly hinge forward at the hips, lowering your torso towards the ground.
4. Pause for a moment at the bottom, then push through your heels to return to the starting position.
5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Sit with your feet wide and flat on the floor, bar on your upper back, and hinge forward from the hips until your chest approaches your thighs.
- The seated position isolates the lower back and hamstrings by removing the legs from the equation.
- Use very light weight since the seated position creates a longer moment arm on the spine.

## Common mistakes

- Rounding the back instead of maintaining a flat spine during the forward hinge.
- Using too much weight because it feels like a small movement, but the spinal loading is significant.
- Not hinging at the hips and instead just rounding the upper back forward.

## FAQ

### How is a Seated Good Morning different from a standing good morning?

The seated version removes leg drive and hip extensor assistance, isolating the lower back and Hamstring Stretch more. It's harder on the spine per pound of weight, so go lighter.

### Is this exercise safe for people with back problems?

It should be avoided if you have existing lower back issues. The seated position combined with a loaded forward hinge puts significant stress on the lumbar spine. Consult a physical therapist first.

### When would I use Seated Good Mornings?

Primarily as a lower back and hamstring strengthening exercise for experienced lifters. Powerlifters use them to build the posterior chain strength needed for heavy squats and Deadlifts.

### What muscles does the Barbell Seated Good Morning work?

The Barbell Seated Good Morning primarily targets the glutes, with secondary activation in the hamstrings, lower back. It's classified as a strength exercise for the legs.

### Is the Barbell Seated Good Morning good for beginners?

The Barbell Seated Good Morning is rated as intermediate. Beginners should build a base of glutes strength with easier variations first.
