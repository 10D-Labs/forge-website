# Dumbbell Standing Bent Over Tricep Extension

## Quick answer

The dumbbell standing bent over tricep extension is an isolation exercise targeting the tricep, performed while bent over and extending the arm behind the body. Intermediate arms exercise using dumbbell. Targets triceps, also works shoulders, back.

- **Body Part:** Arms
- **Equipment:** Dumbbell
- **Target Muscle:** Triceps
- **Secondary Muscles:** Shoulders, Back
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a dumbbell in one hand.
2. Bend forward at the waist, keeping your back straight and parallel to the ground.
3. Extend your arm straight back, keeping your elbow close to your body.
4. Slowly lower the dumbbell back to the starting position.
5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, then switch arms.

## Pro tips

- Hinge forward at the hips until your torso is roughly 45-60 degrees from vertical for optimal resistance angle.
- Pin your upper arm parallel to the floor and extend only at the elbow, driving the dumbbell straight back.
- Pause at full extension and squeeze the tricep before lowering the weight under control.

## Common mistakes

- Standing too upright, which reduces the effective load on the tricep throughout the range of motion.
- Swinging the dumbbell using body momentum, especially as fatigue sets in.
- Dropping the upper arm below parallel, which shortens the range of motion and reduces muscle activation.

## FAQ

### Is the standing version better than seated?

Standing requires more core engagement and balance, which some prefer for functional training. Seated provides more stability and isolation. Both effectively target the triceps.

### How many reps should I do?

Twelve to fifteen reps per arm works well since this is an isolation movement best suited to moderate weight and higher reps.

### Can I do both arms at once?

Yes, but single-arm work allows you to focus on each tricep individually and use slightly more weight per arm. Both approaches are valid.

### What muscles does the Dumbbell Standing Bent Over Tricep Extension work?

The Dumbbell Standing Bent Over Tricep Extension primarily targets the triceps, with secondary activation in the shoulders, back. It's classified as a strength exercise for the arms.

### Is the Dumbbell Standing Bent Over Tricep Extension good for beginners?

The Dumbbell Standing Bent Over Tricep Extension is rated as intermediate. Beginners should build a base of triceps strength with easier variations first.
