# Medicine Ball Catch and Overhead Throw

## Quick answer

The medicine ball catch and overhead throw is a dynamic exercise targeting the lats, shoulders, and core. It involves catching and explosively throwing a medicine ball overhead, combining elements of strength and power. Intermediate back exercise using medicine ball. Targets lats, also works shoulders, core.

- **Body Part:** Back
- **Equipment:** Medicine Ball
- **Target Muscle:** Lats
- **Secondary Muscles:** Shoulders, Core
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Plyometrics

---

## How to perform

1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a medicine ball in both hands at chest level.
2. Bend your knees slightly and engage your core.
3. Lower your body into a squat position, keeping your back straight and chest up.
4. Explosively extend your hips and legs, while simultaneously throwing the medicine ball overhead.
5. Release the ball at the top of the movement and catch it on the way down.
6. Lower your body back into the squat position and repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Use your legs and hips to generate throwing power, not just your arms and shoulders.
- Catch the ball with soft arms and immediately absorb into a quarter squat to dissipate the impact safely.
- Choose a ball weight that allows explosive throws without sacrificing form, typically 6-12 pounds.

## Common mistakes

- Keeping the legs straight and throwing with only the upper body, which limits power and stresses the shoulders.
- Catching the ball with locked elbows, which sends shock through the joints.
- Arching the lower back excessively during the overhead throw instead of engaging the core.

## FAQ

### What type of medicine ball should I use?

Use a non-bouncing (dead) ball for slams or a lightly bouncing rubber ball if you intend to catch it on the rebound. A soft-shell ball is easier on the hands for catching.

### Is this exercise good for building power?

Yes. It trains the stretch-shortening cycle of the lats, shoulders, and core, developing explosive upper body power that transfers to sports.

### Can I do this without a partner?

Yes. Throw the ball against a solid wall and catch the rebound, or throw it straight up and catch it yourself.

### What muscles does the Medicine Ball Catch and Overhead Throw work?

The Medicine Ball Catch and Overhead Throw primarily targets the lats, with secondary activation in the shoulders, core. It's classified as a plyometrics exercise for the back.

### Is the Medicine Ball Catch and Overhead Throw good for beginners?

The Medicine Ball Catch and Overhead Throw is rated as intermediate. Beginners should build a base of lats strength with easier variations first.
