# Elliptical (Distance)

## Quick answer

The elliptical is a cardiovascular exercise performed on an elliptical machine. It simulates a walking or running motion with low impact on the joints, making it suitable for a wide range of fitness levels. The exercise targets the cardiovascular system while also engaging the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and calves. Beginner cardio exercise using cardio machine. - **Body Part:** Cardio
- **Equipment:** Cardio Machine
- **Target Muscle:** Cardiovascular
- **Secondary Muscles:** Quadriceps, Hamstrings, Glutes, Calves
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Cardio

## How to perform

1. Adjust the resistance level and incline of the elliptical machine to your desired settings.
2. Step onto the pedals of the machine and grip the handles lightly.
3. Begin by pushing down with your feet and pulling the handles towards your body.
4. Continue this motion, alternating between pushing and pulling, to simulate a walking or running motion.
5. Maintain a steady pace and keep your core engaged throughout the exercise.
6. Continue until you reach your target distance.
7. Gradually decrease the intensity before stepping off.

## Pro tips

- Push and pull with the handles actively rather than just holding on for the ride to engage your upper body.
- Keep your feet flat on the pedals and drive through your heels to activate the glutes.
- Vary the resistance level throughout your session rather than staying at one setting the entire time.

## Common mistakes

- Holding the stationary handles and letting the legs do all the work, missing the full-body benefit.
- Setting the resistance too low, which allows momentum to do most of the work and reduces calorie burn.
- Leaning forward on the console or handles instead of maintaining an upright posture.

## FAQ

### Is the elliptical as effective as running?

For cardiovascular fitness, yes, when used at comparable intensity. The elliptical is lower impact, making it better for joint health, though it doesn't build bone density as effectively as running.

### What resistance should I use?

Choose a resistance that makes you feel like you're actively pushing and pulling against something, not just spinning your legs. If it feels effortless, increase the resistance.

### Does the elliptical work your upper body?

Only if you actively push and pull the moving handles. Most people just hold on passively. Engage your arms deliberately to make it a true full-body exercise.

### What muscles does the Elliptical (Distance) work?

The Elliptical (Distance) primarily targets the cardiovascular, with secondary activation in the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, calves. It's classified as a cardio exercise for the cardio.

### Is the Elliptical (Distance) good for beginners?

Yes, the Elliptical (Distance) is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building cardiovascular strength.
