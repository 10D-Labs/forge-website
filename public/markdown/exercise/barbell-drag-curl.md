# Barbell Drag Curl - Forge Exercise Library

## Quick Answer

The barbell drag curl is a bicep-focused exercise that involves curling a barbell while keeping the elbows pulled back, emphasizing the contraction of the bicep and minimizing shoulder involvement. It also engages the forearms as secondary muscles.

**Details:**
- **Body Part:** Arms
- **Equipment:** Barbell
- **Target Muscle:** Biceps
- **Secondary Muscles:** Forearms
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to Perform Barbell Drag Curl

1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a barbell with an underhand grip, palms facing up.
2. Let the barbell hang at arm's length in front of your thighs.
3. Keeping your upper arms stationary, curl the barbell up towards your chest by contracting your biceps.
4. Pause for a moment at the top, then slowly lower the barbell back down to the starting position.
5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
