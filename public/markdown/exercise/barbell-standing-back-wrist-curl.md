# Barbell Standing Back Wrist Curl

## Quick answer

The barbell standing back wrist curl is an isolation exercise targeting the forearms, specifically the wrist extensors. It involves curling the barbell upwards using only the wrists while standing. Beginner arms exercise using barbell. Targets forearms, also works biceps, shoulders.

- **Body Part:** Arms
- **Equipment:** Barbell
- **Target Muscle:** Forearms
- **Secondary Muscles:** Biceps, Shoulders
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Stand up straight with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a barbell with an overhand grip.
2. Rest the barbell on the back of your hands with your palms facing down and your fingers pointing towards your body.
3. Keeping your upper arms stationary, exhale and curl your wrists upwards as far as possible.
4. Hold the contracted position for a brief pause, then inhale and slowly lower the barbell back to the starting position.
5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Hold the bar behind your body with palms facing backward and curl your wrists upward by contracting the forearm extensors.
- Keep the movement small and controlled; this targets a small muscle group that doesn't need big range of motion.
- Stand upright and keep your upper arms and elbows stationary throughout the movement.

## Common mistakes

- Using too much weight and compensating by moving the entire arm instead of isolating the wrists.
- Performing the movement too quickly, which uses momentum and reduces the training effect on the wrist extensors.
- Bending the elbows or shrugging the shoulders to assist the movement.

## FAQ

### What's the difference between a back wrist curl and a regular wrist curl?

A back wrist curl has the bar behind your body with palms facing backward, targeting the wrist extensors. A regular wrist curl has the bar in front with palms up, targeting the wrist flexors.

### Why train the muscles on the back of the wrist?

Strong wrist extensors prevent imbalances that lead to tennis elbow and wrist pain. They also improve grip endurance for exercises like Deadlifts, rows, and Pullups.

### How much weight should I use?

Very light, usually 15-30 lbs. The wrist extensors are small muscles. Focus on 15-20 controlled reps rather than heavy weight.

### What muscles does the Barbell Standing Back Wrist Curl work?

The Barbell Standing Back Wrist Curl primarily targets the forearms, with secondary activation in the biceps, shoulders. It's classified as a strength exercise for the arms.

### Is the Barbell Standing Back Wrist Curl good for beginners?

Yes, the Barbell Standing Back Wrist Curl is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building forearms strength.
