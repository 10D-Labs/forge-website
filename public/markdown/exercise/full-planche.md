# Full Planche

## Quick answer

The full planche is an advanced bodyweight exercise that requires significant core strength, balance, and upper body control. It involves holding your body parallel to the ground, supported only by your hands, with no other contact points. Advanced core exercise using body weight. Targets abs, also works shoulders, chest, triceps.

- **Body Part:** Core
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Abs
- **Secondary Muscles:** Shoulders, Chest, Triceps
- **Difficulty:** Advanced
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Start in a pushup position with your hands shoulder-width apart and your fingers pointing forward.
2. Engage your core and slowly shift your weight forward, lifting your feet off the ground.
3. Continue shifting your weight forward until your body is parallel to the ground, balancing on your hands.
4. Hold this position for as long as you can, maintaining a straight body line.
5. Slowly lower your feet back to the ground and return to the starting position.
6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Master the Straddle Planche for at least 10 seconds before attempting to close your legs for a Full Planche.
- Protract your shoulder blades maximally and lean your shoulders past your wrists to find the balance point.
- Train planche leans and tuck planche Pushups regularly to build the specific shoulder strength required.

## Common mistakes

- Attempting the Full Planche without sufficient time in easier progressions, leading to shoulder or wrist injuries.
- Piking at the hips (butt rising up), which shortens the lever arm and isn't a true Full Planche.
- Training the planche daily without adequate rest, which leads to chronic tendonitis in the wrists and elbows.

## FAQ

### Is the Full Planche the hardest calisthenics hold?

It's among the hardest. The Full Maltese and Victorian cross on rings are considered more difficult, but the Full Planche is the benchmark for elite calisthenics strength.

### How heavy is too heavy for a Full Planche?

There's no hard cutoff, but athletes over 180 lbs face significantly greater difficulty. Most planche specialists are under 170 lbs.

### Can I train planche every day?

No. The wrists, elbows, and shoulders need recovery. Train planche-specific work 3-4 times per week with at least one rest day between sessions.

### What muscles does the Full Planche work?

The Full Planche primarily targets the abs, with secondary activation in the shoulders, chest, triceps. It's classified as a strength exercise for the core.

### Is the Full Planche good for beginners?

The Full Planche is an advanced exercise. Beginners should master fundamental core movements first.
