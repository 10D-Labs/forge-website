# Pelvic Tilt

## Quick answer

The pelvic tilt is a bodyweight exercise that targets the abdominal muscles and helps improve core stability and lower back health. It is often used in rehabilitation and as a foundational movement for beginners. Beginner core exercise using body weight. Targets abs, also works lower back.

- **Body Part:** Core
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Abs
- **Secondary Muscles:** Lower Back
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Rehabilitation

---

## How to perform

1. Lie flat on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the ground.
2. Place your hands by your sides.
3. Engage your abs and tilt your pelvis upward, pressing your lower back into the ground.
4. Hold this position for a few seconds, focusing on contracting your abs.
5. Release the tilt and return to the starting position.
6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Focus on flattening your lower back completely against the floor by engaging your deep lower abs.
- Hold the tilted position for 5-10 seconds per rep to build endurance in the core stabilizers.
- Breathe normally during the hold; do not hold your breath.

## Common mistakes

- Using the glutes to push the hips up instead of engaging the lower abs to tilt the pelvis.
- Holding the breath, which reduces the ability to maintain the contraction for extended holds.
- Performing the movement too quickly without holding the tilt, which reduces the isometric strengthening benefit.

## FAQ

### What is the Pelvic Tilt exercise good for?

It teaches you to engage the deep core muscles and control pelvic position, which is foundational for safe performance of nearly all other exercises.

### Can Pelvic Tilts help lower back pain?

Yes, Pelvic Tilts are one of the most commonly prescribed exercises for lower back pain because they gently strengthen the core and improve pelvic awareness without loading the spine.

### How often should I do Pelvic Tilts?

Daily is fine, especially if you have lower back pain or poor core awareness. Aim for 3 sets of 10-15 reps with 5-10 second holds.

### What muscles does the Pelvic Tilt work?

The Pelvic Tilt primarily targets the abs, with secondary activation in the lower back. It's classified as a rehabilitation exercise for the core.

### Is the Pelvic Tilt good for beginners?

Yes, the Pelvic Tilt is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building abs strength.
