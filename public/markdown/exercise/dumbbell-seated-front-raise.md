# Dumbbell Seated Front Raise - Forge Exercise Library

## Quick Answer

The dumbbell seated front raise is a shoulder exercise that targets the deltoid muscles. It is performed while seated, which helps to isolate the shoulders and minimize momentum, making it a controlled movement for building shoulder strength.

**Details:**
- **Body Part:** Shoulders
- **Equipment:** Dumbbell
- **Target Muscle:** Shoulders
- **Secondary Muscles:** Trapezius, Biceps
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to Perform Dumbbell Seated Front Raise

1. Sit on a bench with your feet flat on the ground and a dumbbell in each hand, resting on your thighs.
2. Keep your back straight and core engaged.
3. Raise the dumbbells in front of you, with your palms facing down, until they are at shoulder level.
4. Pause for a moment at the top, then slowly lower the dumbbells back down to the starting position.
5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
