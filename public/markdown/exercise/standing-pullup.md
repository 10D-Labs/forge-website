# Standing Pullup

## Quick answer

The assisted standing pullup is a machine-based exercise targeting the latissimus dorsi (lats) with assistance to help perform the pullup movement. It is suitable for those building strength to perform unassisted pullup. Beginner back exercise using machine. Targets lats, also works biceps, forearms.

- **Body Part:** Back
- **Equipment:** Machine
- **Target Muscle:** Lats
- **Secondary Muscles:** Biceps, Forearms
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Adjust the machine to your desired weight and height settings.
2. Stand facing the machine with your feet shoulder-width apart.
3. Grasp the handles with an overhand grip, slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
4. Engage your lats and biceps, and pull yourself up towards the handles.
5. Pause for a moment at the top, squeezing your back muscles.
6. Slowly lower yourself back down to the starting position.
7. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Use a pronated (palms away) grip slightly wider than shoulder width for standard lat emphasis.
- Focus on initiating the pull by depressing and retracting the shoulder blades before bending the elbows.
- Reduce the assistance weight progressively each session to build toward unassisted Pullups.

## Common mistakes

- Relying on too much machine assistance for too long, which limits strength progression.
- Pulling primarily with the arms rather than engaging the lats and back muscles first.
- Using a partial range of motion by not lowering fully to straight arms between reps.

## FAQ

### How much assistance should I start with?

Start with about 40-50% of your bodyweight as assistance. Aim for 3 sets of 8-12 reps. If you can do more than 12 easily, reduce the assistance.

### Is the Assisted Pullup machine as effective as real Pullups?

It builds the same muscles but the fixed path and counterbalance make it less demanding on stabilizers. It is an excellent tool for building the strength needed for unassisted Pullups.

### How long does it take to progress to unassisted Pullups?

Depends on starting strength and bodyweight, but most people can progress from fully assisted to unassisted Pullups in 8-16 weeks with consistent training 2-3 times per week.

### What muscles does the Standing Pullup work?

The Standing Pullup primarily targets the lats, with secondary activation in the biceps, forearms. It's classified as a strength exercise for the back.

### Is the Standing Pullup good for beginners?

Yes, the Standing Pullup is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building lats strength.
