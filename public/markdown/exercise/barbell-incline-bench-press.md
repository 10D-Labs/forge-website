# Barbell Incline Bench Press - Forge Exercise Library

## Quick Answer

The barbell incline bench press is a compound upper-body exercise that targets the upper portion of the pectoral muscles, with secondary emphasis on the shoulders and tricep. It is performed on an incline bench using a barbell, making it a staple in strength training routines for developing chest size and strength.

**Details:**
- **Body Part:** Chest
- **Equipment:** Barbell
- **Target Muscle:** Chest
- **Secondary Muscles:** Shoulders, Triceps
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to Perform Barbell Incline Bench Press

1. Set up an incline bench at a 45 degree angle.
2. Lie down on the bench with your feet flat on the ground.
3. Grasp the barbell with an overhand grip, slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
4. Unrack the barbell and lower it slowly towards your chest, keeping your elbows at a 45 degree angle.
5. Pause for a moment at the bottom, then push the barbell back up to the starting position.
6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
