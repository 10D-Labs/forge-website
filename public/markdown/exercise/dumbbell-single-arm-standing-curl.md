# Dumbbell Single Arm Standing Curl - Forge Exercise Library

## Quick Answer

The dumbbell one arm standing curl is a classic isolation exercise targeting the bicep. It involves curling a dumbbell with one arm while standing, focusing on strict form to maximize bicep engagement.

**Details:**
- **Body Part:** Arms
- **Equipment:** Dumbbell
- **Target Muscle:** Biceps
- **Secondary Muscles:** Forearms
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to Perform Dumbbell Single Arm Standing Curl

1. Stand up straight with a dumbbell in one hand, palm facing forward and arm fully extended.
2. Keeping your upper arm stationary, exhale and curl the weight upward while contracting your biceps.
3. Continue to raise the dumbbell until your biceps are fully contracted and the dumbbell is at shoulder level.
4. Hold the contracted position for a brief pause as you squeeze your biceps.
5. Inhale and slowly lower the dumbbell back to the starting position.
6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, then switch arms.
