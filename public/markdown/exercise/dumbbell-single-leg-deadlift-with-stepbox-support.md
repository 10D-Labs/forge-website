# Dumbbell Single Leg Deadlift with Stepbox Support

## Quick answer

The dumbbell single leg deadlift with stepbox support is a unilateral lower body exercise that targets the glutes, with secondary emphasis on the hamstrings and lower back. It challenges balance, stability, and strength, making it suitable for those with some experience in weight training. Intermediate legs exercise using dumbbell. Targets glutes, also works hamstrings, lower back.

- **Body Part:** Legs
- **Equipment:** Dumbbell
- **Target Muscle:** Glutes
- **Secondary Muscles:** Hamstrings, Lower Back
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Stand with your feet hip-width apart, holding a dumbbell in your right hand.
2. Place your left foot on a stepbox or elevated surface behind you.
3. Keeping your back straight and core engaged, hinge forward at the hips, lowering the dumbbell towards the ground.
4. As you lower the dumbbell, simultaneously lift your left leg behind you, maintaining a straight line from head to heel.
5. Lower the dumbbell until you feel a stretch in your right hamstring, then return to the starting position.
6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, then switch sides.

## Pro tips

- Use the step box as a balance reference point, not a crutch. Your back foot should rest lightly on it.
- Hinge at the hips and push your elevated foot backward as you lower the dumbbell, keeping your back flat.
- Focus on feeling a deep stretch in the hamstring of the standing leg before reversing the movement.

## Common mistakes

- Rounding the back during the hinge, which puts the lumbar spine at risk under load.
- Placing too much weight on the back foot on the step box, which defeats the single-leg training purpose.
- Rotating the hips open on the standing-leg side instead of keeping them square to the floor.

## FAQ

### Why use a step box for single leg Deadlifts?

The step box provides a balance reference for the back foot, making the exercise more accessible while still training single-leg hip hinge strength. It is a useful progression between bilateral and full single-leg Deadlifts.

### How heavy should I go on single leg Deadlifts?

Start with a light dumbbell (10-20 lbs) and progress slowly. Balance is the limiting factor for most people, not strength.

### What muscles does the single leg Deadlift work?

It primarily targets the glutes and hamstrings of the standing leg, with significant work on the lower back, core, and hip stabilizers for balance.

### What muscles does the Dumbbell Single Leg Deadlift with Stepbox Support work?

The Dumbbell Single Leg Deadlift with Stepbox Support primarily targets the glutes, with secondary activation in the hamstrings, lower back. It's classified as a strength exercise for the legs.

### Is the Dumbbell Single Leg Deadlift with Stepbox Support good for beginners?

The Dumbbell Single Leg Deadlift with Stepbox Support is rated as intermediate. Beginners should build a base of glutes strength with easier variations first.
