# Butt Up - Forge Exercise Library

## Quick Answer

Butt-ups are a bodyweight exercise targeting the abdominal muscles. The movement involves lying on your back, lifting your legs towards your chest, and engaging your core to raise your hips off the ground. This exercise also works the hip flexors and lower back as secondary muscles.

**Details:**
- **Body Part:** Core
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Abs
- **Secondary Muscles:** Hip Flexors, Lower Back
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to Perform Butt Up

1. Lie flat on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the ground.
2. Place your hands by your sides, palms facing down.
3. Engaging your abs, lift your legs off the ground, bringing your knees towards your chest.
4. At the top of the movement, squeeze your abs and pause for a moment.
5. Slowly lower your legs back down to the starting position.
6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
