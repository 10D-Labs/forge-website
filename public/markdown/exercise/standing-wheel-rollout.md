# Standing Wheel Rollout

## Quick answer

The standing wheel rollout is an advanced core exercise that primarily targets the abdominal muscles, while also engaging the lower back and shoulders. It requires significant core strength, balance, and control to perform safely and effectively. Advanced core exercise using ab wheel. Targets abs, also works lower back, shoulders.

- **Body Part:** Core
- **Equipment:** Ab Wheel
- **Target Muscle:** Abs
- **Secondary Muscles:** Lower Back, Shoulders
- **Difficulty:** Advanced
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Start by standing tall with your feet shoulder-width apart and the wheel roller in front of you.
2. Bend at your waist and slowly roll the wheel forward, keeping your back straight and your core engaged.
3. Continue rolling forward until you feel a stretch in your abs and your body is extended as far as possible.
4. Pause for a moment, then slowly roll the wheel back towards your body, returning to the starting position.
5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Start with a narrow stance and roll out only as far as you can while maintaining a flat lower back.
- Brace your abs hard before rolling out, as if you're about to take a punch to the stomach.
- Pull the wheel back by engaging your abs and lats, not by bending at the hips.

## Common mistakes

- Letting the lower back sag into Hyperextension during the rollout, which can cause serious lower back pain.
- Rolling out too far beyond your current core strength, which collapses the form and strains the lower back.
- Using the hip flexors to pull back instead of the abdominals.

## FAQ

### Is the standing ab rollout harder than kneeling?

Dramatically harder. The standing version is an elite core exercise that many strong individuals cannot perform with full range of motion. Master the kneeling version for 15+ reps before attempting standing.

### Why does my lower back hurt during standing rollouts?

Your core isn't strong enough to maintain a neutral spine through the full range of motion. Regress to kneeling rollouts or reduce the rollout distance. The lower back should never arch during this exercise.

### How do I progress to the full standing rollout?

Start with kneeling rollouts, then standing rollouts to a wall (limiting your range of motion), then gradually increase the distance by moving further from the wall until you can do a full standing rollout.

### What muscles does the Standing Wheel Rollout work?

The Standing Wheel Rollout primarily targets the abs, with secondary activation in the lower back, shoulders. It's classified as a strength exercise for the core.

### Is the Standing Wheel Rollout good for beginners?

The Standing Wheel Rollout is an advanced exercise. Beginners should master fundamental core movements first.
