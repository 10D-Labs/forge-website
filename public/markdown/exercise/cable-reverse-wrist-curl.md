# Cable Reverse Wrist Curl - Forge Exercise Library

## Quick Answer

The cable reverse wrist curl is an isolation exercise targeting the forearms, specifically the wrist extensors. It is performed using a cable machine and focuses on strengthening the muscles responsible for wrist extension.

**Details:**
- **Body Part:** Arms
- **Equipment:** Cable
- **Target Muscle:** Forearms
- **Secondary Muscles:** Forearms, Wrists
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to Perform Cable Reverse Wrist Curl

1. Attach a cable to a low pulley and sit on a bench facing the cable machine.
2. Grasp the cable handle with an overhand grip, palms facing down.
3. Rest your forearms on your thighs, with your wrists hanging off the edge.
4. Keeping your forearms stationary, exhale and curl your wrists upward as far as possible.
5. Pause for a moment at the top, then inhale and slowly lower your wrists back down to the starting position.
6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
