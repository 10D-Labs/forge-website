# Dumbbell Single Arm Reverse Grip Press

## Quick answer

The dumbbell one arm reverse grip press is a unilateral chest exercise that targets the pectorals with a unique grip, also engaging the tricep and shoulders. It requires stability and control due to the reverse grip and single arm execution. Intermediate chest exercise using dumbbell. Targets chest, also works triceps, shoulders.

- **Body Part:** Chest
- **Equipment:** Dumbbell
- **Target Muscle:** Chest
- **Secondary Muscles:** Triceps, Shoulders
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Sit on a flat bench with a dumbbell in one hand, palm facing towards your body.
2. Place your feet flat on the ground and keep your back straight.
3. Raise the dumbbell to shoulder height, keeping your elbow close to your body.
4. Press the dumbbell upwards until your arm is fully extended.
5. Pause for a moment at the top, then slowly lower the dumbbell back to the starting position.
6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, then switch to the other arm.

## Pro tips

- Start with the palm facing toward you (supinated grip) and maintain this position throughout the entire press.
- Keep the pressing elbow at about a 45-degree angle to your torso, not fully flared or fully tucked.
- Lower the dumbbell under control until it lightly touches the outer edge of your chest before pressing back up.

## Common mistakes

- Letting the wrist rotate during the press, losing the reverse grip position and changing the muscle emphasis.
- Using too heavy a weight before mastering the unfamiliar grip, which can lead to the dumbbell slipping.
- Pressing the dumbbell too far over the face instead of over the chest, which stresses the shoulder.

## FAQ

### What does the reverse grip do for the Chest Press?

The supinated grip shifts emphasis to the upper chest fibers and increases bicep involvement during the press. It also tends to be more shoulder-friendly for people with anterior shoulder pain.

### Is the reverse grip press safe for the wrists?

Yes, as long as you keep the wrist neutral and don't bend it backward under load. Start light and increase weight only as the grip pattern feels natural.

### Can I do this on a flat bench or does it need to be incline?

This exercise is typically done on a flat bench. The reverse grip itself provides upper-chest emphasis, so an incline isn't necessary but can be added for variation.

### What muscles does the Dumbbell Single Arm Reverse Grip Press work?

The Dumbbell Single Arm Reverse Grip Press primarily targets the chest, with secondary activation in the triceps, shoulders. It's classified as a strength exercise for the chest.

### Is the Dumbbell Single Arm Reverse Grip Press good for beginners?

The Dumbbell Single Arm Reverse Grip Press is rated as intermediate. Beginners should build a base of chest strength with easier variations first.
