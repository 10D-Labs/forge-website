# Dumbbell Standing Palms in Press

## Quick answer

The dumbbell standing palms in press is a shoulder exercise that targets the deltoids, with secondary emphasis on the tricep and chest. It involves pressing dumbbells overhead while keeping the palms facing inward, which can be easier on the shoulder joints compared to a traditional overhead press. Beginner shoulders exercise using dumbbell. Targets shoulders, also works triceps, chest.

- **Body Part:** Shoulders
- **Equipment:** Dumbbell
- **Target Muscle:** Shoulders
- **Secondary Muscles:** Triceps, Chest
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand at shoulder level with your palms facing inwards.
2. Keeping your core engaged and your back straight, press the dumbbells upwards until your arms are fully extended overhead.
3. Pause for a moment at the top, then slowly lower the dumbbells back to the starting position.
4. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Press both dumbbells overhead with palms facing each other (neutral grip) for a shoulder-friendly pressing pattern.
- Keep the elbows slightly in front of the body rather than flared to the sides for a more natural pressing arc.
- Lock out fully at the top with the dumbbells nearly touching above your head.

## Common mistakes

- Rotating the palms to face forward during the press, negating the neutral grip benefit.
- Arching the back excessively, especially as fatigue sets in during later reps.
- Not pressing to full lockout, which shortchanges the triceps involvement at the top of the movement.

## FAQ

### Is the palms-in press the same as the Arnold press?

No. The Arnold press includes a rotation from palms-in to palms-out during the press. The palms-in press keeps the neutral grip throughout.

### Why choose palms-in over regular overhead press?

The neutral grip reduces shoulder joint stress and can be more comfortable for people with shoulder impingement. It also slightly increases triceps involvement.

### Can I build big shoulders with palms-in press?

Absolutely. The shoulders work hard regardless of grip orientation. The palms-in grip just changes the emphasis slightly and improves joint comfort.

### What muscles does the Dumbbell Standing Palms in Press work?

The Dumbbell Standing Palms in Press primarily targets the shoulders, with secondary activation in the triceps, chest. It's classified as a strength exercise for the shoulders.

### Is the Dumbbell Standing Palms in Press good for beginners?

Yes, the Dumbbell Standing Palms in Press is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building shoulders strength.
