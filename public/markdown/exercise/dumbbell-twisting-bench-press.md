# Dumbbell Twisting Bench Press

## Quick answer

The dumbbell twisting bench press is a variation of the standard dumbbell bench press that incorporates a wrist twist to further engage the tricep, chest, and shoulders. This exercise requires coordination and control to safely twist the wrists during the pressing motion. Intermediate arms exercise using dumbbell. Targets triceps, also works chest, shoulders.

- **Body Part:** Arms
- **Equipment:** Dumbbell
- **Target Muscle:** Triceps
- **Secondary Muscles:** Chest, Shoulders
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Lie flat on a bench with your feet flat on the ground and your back pressed against the bench.
2. Hold a dumbbell in each hand with an overhand grip, palms facing away from you.
3. Extend your arms straight up over your chest, keeping a slight bend in your elbows.
4. Lower the dumbbells down towards your chest, keeping your elbows close to your body.
5. As you lower the dumbbells, twist your wrists so that your palms face towards you at the bottom of the movement.
6. Pause for a moment at the bottom, then reverse the movement by pressing the dumbbells back up to the starting position.
7. As you press the dumbbells up, twist your wrists back to the starting position with palms facing away from you.
8. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Start with palms facing away from you and rotate to palms facing you as you lower the dumbbells.
- Reverse the twist as you press back up so your palms face away at the top again.
- The twisting motion should be smooth and coordinated with the pressing movement, not jerky.

## Common mistakes

- Twisting too aggressively, which can strain the wrist or shoulder joints.
- Losing the coordination between the twist and the press, creating an uneven movement pattern.
- Using too much weight, which makes the rotation feel unnatural and risky for the shoulder joint.

## FAQ

### What does the twist add to a bench press?

The rotation engages the triceps through pronation and supination, adds variety to the pressing pattern, and can target different portions of the chest and triceps depending on the rotation angle.

### Is this exercise safe for the shoulders?

Yes, when done with moderate weight and controlled rotation. The dumbbell version is safer than barbell variations because the wrists can rotate naturally.

### How much weight should I use?

Start with about 60-70% of your regular Dumbbell Bench Press weight. The rotation adds complexity, so lighter weight with good control is more effective than going heavy.

### What muscles does the Dumbbell Twisting Bench Press work?

The Dumbbell Twisting Bench Press primarily targets the triceps, with secondary activation in the chest, shoulders. It's classified as a strength exercise for the arms.

### Is the Dumbbell Twisting Bench Press good for beginners?

The Dumbbell Twisting Bench Press is rated as intermediate. Beginners should build a base of triceps strength with easier variations first.
