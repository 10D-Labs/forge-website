# Chest Dip on Straight Bar

## Quick answer

The chest dip on a straight bar is a bodyweight exercise that targets the pectoral muscles, with secondary emphasis on the tricep and shoulders. It requires significant upper body strength, stability, and control. Advanced chest exercise using body weight. Targets chest, also works triceps, shoulders.

- **Body Part:** Chest
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Chest
- **Secondary Muscles:** Triceps, Shoulders
- **Difficulty:** Advanced
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Grab the parallel bars with your palms facing down and your arms fully extended.
2. Bend your knees and cross your ankles.
3. Lower your body by bending your arms until your shoulders are below your elbows.
4. Push yourself back up to the starting position by straightening your arms.
5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Lean forward about 30 degrees over the bar to shift emphasis to the chest rather than the triceps.
- Lower yourself until your shoulders are at or slightly below elbow level for a full chest stretch.
- Keep your legs slightly forward of your body to help maintain the forward lean.

## Common mistakes

- Staying upright during the dip, which makes it a tricep exercise and reduces chest involvement.
- Dropping too fast into the bottom position, which can strain the shoulders and sternum.
- Not going deep enough, which limits the stretch on the chest and reduces muscle activation.

## FAQ

### Why is the straight bar Chest Dip harder than parallel bar dips?

The straight bar requires more wrist mobility and stabilization since both hands are on the same bar. It also demands more shoulder control to maintain balance.

### Is the Chest Dip on a straight bar safe for shoulders?

It is safe with proper form and controlled speed, but people with shoulder impingement or instability should avoid this advanced variation.

### How do I progress to straight bar dips?

Master parallel bar dips for 3 sets of 10+ reps first, then gradually practice the straight bar version with partial range of motion before going full depth.

### What muscles does the Chest Dip on Straight Bar work?

The Chest Dip on Straight Bar primarily targets the chest, with secondary activation in the triceps, shoulders. It's classified as a strength exercise for the chest.

### Is the Chest Dip on Straight Bar good for beginners?

The Chest Dip on Straight Bar is an advanced exercise. Beginners should master fundamental chest movements first.
