# Hands Bike (Time)

## Quick answer

The hands bike is a cardiovascular exercise that targets the pectorals, triceps, and shoulders by requiring the user to pedal with their hands, simulating a cycling motion for the upper body. Beginner cardio exercise using cardio machine. Targets cardiovascular, also works triceps, shoulders.

- **Body Part:** Cardio
- **Equipment:** Cardio Machine
- **Target Muscle:** Cardiovascular
- **Secondary Muscles:** Triceps, Shoulders
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Cardio

---

## How to perform

1. Adjust the seat height and handlebar position to a comfortable level.
2. Sit on the ergometer with your back straight and feet on the pedals.
3. Grasp the handles with your hands and position your arms at a 90 degree angle.
4. Start pedaling with your hands, pushing and pulling the handles in a controlled motion.
5. Continue pedaling for your target duration.

## Pro tips

- Keep your back straight against the seat pad and avoid hunching forward as you fatigue.
- Focus on pushing and pulling evenly with both arms to prevent muscle imbalances.
- Start at a low resistance and moderate pace to warm up, then increase intensity after 2-3 minutes.

## Common mistakes

- Gripping the handles too tightly, which causes premature forearm fatigue and takes away from the cardio benefit.
- Using only a pushing motion rather than a balanced push-pull, which neglects the pulling muscles and reduces calorie burn.
- Setting the seat too high or low, which forces awkward arm angles and strains the shoulders.

## FAQ

### What is a hand bike good for?

An upper body ergometer provides cardiovascular exercise for people who cannot use their lower body, or as a warm-up and active recovery tool for the shoulders and arms.

### How many calories does a hand bike burn?

Roughly 150-300 calories per 30 minutes depending on intensity, though this is typically less than lower body cardio since smaller muscle groups are involved.

### Is the hand bike good for shoulder rehab?

Yes, at low resistance it promotes blood flow to the shoulder joint and gently strengthens the rotator cuff and surrounding muscles without heavy loading.

### What muscles does the Hands Bike (Time) work?

The Hands Bike (Time) primarily targets the cardiovascular, with secondary activation in the triceps, shoulders. It's classified as a cardio exercise for the cardio.

### Is the Hands Bike (Time) good for beginners?

Yes, the Hands Bike (Time) is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building cardiovascular strength.
