# Side Lying Adductor Stretch

## Quick answer

The assisted side lying adductor stretch is a flexibility exercise targeting the inner thigh muscles (adductors), performed while lying on your side and using a bench or step for assistance. Beginner legs exercise using machine. Targets adductors, also works hamstrings, glutes.

- **Body Part:** Legs
- **Equipment:** Machine
- **Target Muscle:** Adductors
- **Secondary Muscles:** Hamstrings, Glutes
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Stretching

---

## How to perform

1. Lie on your side with your legs straight and stacked on top of each other.
2. Bend your bottom leg slightly for stability.
3. Place your top foot on a stable surface, such as a bench or step.
4. Keeping your top leg straight, slowly lower it towards the ground, feeling a stretch in your inner thigh.
5. Hold the stretch for 20-30 seconds.
6. Return to the starting position and repeat on the other side.

## Pro tips

- Keep your top leg straight and lower it slowly toward the floor to control the stretch intensity on the inner thigh.
- Stabilize your body by engaging your core and keeping your bottom leg slightly bent for balance.
- Hold at the deepest comfortable point for 20-30 seconds before returning to the starting position.

## Common mistakes

- Rolling forward or backward during the stretch, which changes the angle and reduces adductor targeting.
- Lowering the leg too fast, which can strain the adductor muscles if they're not warmed up.
- Not using a stable elevated surface, which makes the exercise ineffective and potentially unsafe.

## FAQ

### What are the adductors and why should I stretch them?

The adductors are the inner thigh muscles that pull your legs together. Stretching them improves hip mobility, squat depth, and reduces the risk of groin strains.

### Can I do this stretch without a bench?

You can modify it by lying on your side and simply lowering your top leg toward the floor in front of you. A bench or step allows more range of motion.

### Is this stretch good before squats?

A gentle version can be part of a warmup, but deep static holds are better saved for after training. Before squats, use dynamic adductor stretches like lateral lunges instead.

### What muscles does the Side Lying Adductor Stretch work?

The Side Lying Adductor Stretch primarily targets the adductors, with secondary activation in the hamstrings, glutes. It's classified as a stretching exercise for the legs.

### Is the Side Lying Adductor Stretch good for beginners?

Yes, the Side Lying Adductor Stretch is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building adductors strength.
