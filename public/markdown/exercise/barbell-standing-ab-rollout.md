# Barbell Standing Ab Rollout

## Quick answer

The barbell standing ab rollout is an advanced core exercise that targets the abs, with secondary emphasis on the shoulders and lower back. It requires significant core strength, stability, and control to perform safely and effectively. Advanced core exercise using barbell. Targets abs, also works shoulders, lower back.

- **Body Part:** Core
- **Equipment:** Barbell
- **Target Muscle:** Abs
- **Secondary Muscles:** Shoulders, Lower Back
- **Difficulty:** Advanced
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Stand upright with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold the barbell with both hands in front of your thighs.
2. Engage your core and slowly roll the barbell down towards the ground, keeping your back straight and your arms extended.
3. Continue rolling the barbell forward until your body is fully extended and your hands are directly above your head.
4. Pause for a moment, then slowly roll the barbell back towards your thighs, maintaining control and keeping your core engaged.
5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- This is an extremely advanced exercise; you should be able to do 15+ kneeling rollouts with perfect form before attempting standing rollouts.
- Start from a standing position, bend forward, and roll the bar out along the floor until your body is nearly parallel to the ground.
- Keep your glutes squeezed and core braced to prevent your lower back from collapsing at full extension.

## Common mistakes

- Attempting this exercise without sufficient core strength, leading to lower back collapse and potential injury.
- Letting the hips sag and the lower back hyperextend at full extension.
- Bending at the hips on the way back up instead of pulling through the core to return to standing.

## FAQ

### How hard is a standing Barbell Rollout compared to kneeling?

Dramatically harder. The longer lever arm and greater range of motion require elite-level core strength. Most experienced lifters cannot perform even one rep from standing with proper form.

### What should I do if I can't do a full standing rollout?

Progress through these stages: kneeling rollout, kneeling rollout with extended reach, rollout from a slight elevation (step), and finally standing rollout. Each stage builds the strength needed for the next.

### Can standing rollouts cause back injury?

Yes, if your core isn't strong enough to maintain a neutral spine at full extension. The lumbar spine bears tremendous shear force during this exercise. Only attempt it when you've mastered kneeling rollouts.

### What muscles does the Barbell Standing Ab Rollout work?

The Barbell Standing Ab Rollout primarily targets the abs, with secondary activation in the shoulders, lower back. It's classified as a strength exercise for the core.

### Is the Barbell Standing Ab Rollout good for beginners?

The Barbell Standing Ab Rollout is an advanced exercise. Beginners should master fundamental core movements first.
