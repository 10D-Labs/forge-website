# Dumbbell Single Arm Reverse Preacher Curl - Forge Exercise Library

## Quick Answer

The dumbbell one arm reverse preacher curl is an isolation exercise targeting the bicep, performed on a preacher bench with a single dumbbell. It emphasizes strict form and control, helping to build bicep and forearm strength.

**Details:**
- **Body Part:** Arms
- **Equipment:** Dumbbell
- **Target Muscle:** Biceps
- **Secondary Muscles:** Forearms
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to Perform Dumbbell Single Arm Reverse Preacher Curl

1. Sit on a preacher bench with your chest against the pad and your arm extended over the edge of the bench, holding a dumbbell with an underhand grip.
2. Lower the dumbbell slowly until your arm is fully extended.
3. Curl the dumbbell back up towards your shoulder, keeping your upper arm stationary.
4. Squeeze your biceps at the top of the movement, then slowly lower the dumbbell back down to the starting position.
5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, then switch arms.
