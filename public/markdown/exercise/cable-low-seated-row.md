# Cable Low Seated Row - Forge Exercise Library

## Quick Answer

The cable low seated row is a strength exercise targeting the upper back, performed on a cable machine. It also engages the bicep and forearms as secondary muscles. The movement involves pulling the cable handles toward the torso while maintaining a straight back and squeezing the shoulder blades together.

**Details:**
- **Body Part:** Back
- **Equipment:** Cable
- **Target Muscle:** Upper Back
- **Secondary Muscles:** Biceps, Forearms
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to Perform Cable Low Seated Row

1. Sit on the machine with your feet flat on the footrests and your knees slightly bent.
2. Grasp the handles with an overhand grip, palms facing down.
3. Keep your back straight and lean slightly forward, maintaining a slight bend in your elbows.
4. Pull the handles towards your body, squeezing your shoulder blades together.
5. Pause for a moment at the peak of the movement, then slowly release the handles back to the starting position.
6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
