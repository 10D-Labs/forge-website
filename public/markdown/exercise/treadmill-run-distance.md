# Treadmill Run (Distance)

## Quick answer

Treadmill running is a cardiovascular exercise performed on a motorized treadmill machine. It allows for controlled pacing, incline adjustments, and weather-independent training, making it ideal for consistent cardio workouts. Beginner cardio exercise using treadmill. Targets cardiovascular, also works quadriceps, hamstrings, calves, glutes.

- **Body Part:** Cardio
- **Equipment:** Treadmill
- **Target Muscle:** Cardiovascular
- **Secondary Muscles:** Quadriceps, Hamstrings, Calves, Glutes
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Cardio

---

## How to perform

1. Step onto the treadmill and straddle the belt, placing your feet on the side rails.
2. Attach the safety clip to your clothing if available.
3. Start the treadmill at a slow walking pace to warm up.
4. Gradually increase the speed to your desired running pace.
5. Maintain an upright posture with your arms swinging naturally at your sides.
6. Land with a midfoot strike and push off with your toes.
7. Continue running until you reach your target distance.
8. Gradually decrease speed to cool down, then carefully step off the treadmill.

## Pro tips

- Set the incline to 1% to simulate outdoor running conditions and compensate for the lack of wind resistance.
- Don't grip the handrails while running; pump your arms naturally for proper running mechanics.
- Cover the display with a towel if you find yourself watching the distance count up, which can make the run feel longer.

## Common mistakes

- Holding onto the handrails, which alters your running gait and reduces calorie burn significantly.
- Setting the speed too high initially and having to stop or hold on, rather than building up gradually.
- Running with a zero incline, which doesn't replicate the energy cost of outdoor running and shortens the hamstrings.

## FAQ

### Is treadmill running easier than outdoor running?

Slightly, because the belt assists leg turnover and there's no wind resistance. Setting a 1% incline equalizes the effort to be comparable to flat outdoor running.

### What speed should I set the treadmill to?

Your easy pace outdoors is a good starting point. Most recreational runners do easy runs between 5.0-6.5 mph. Use the talk test to gauge your effort.

### Does treadmill running translate to outdoor running performance?

Yes, the cardiovascular and muscular adaptations transfer well. However, outdoor running also develops proprioception and terrain adaptation that treadmills don't.

### What muscles does the Treadmill Run (Distance) work?

The Treadmill Run (Distance) primarily targets the cardiovascular, with secondary activation in the quadriceps, hamstrings, calves, glutes. It's classified as a cardio exercise for the cardio.

### Is the Treadmill Run (Distance) good for beginners?

Yes, the Treadmill Run (Distance) is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building cardiovascular strength.
