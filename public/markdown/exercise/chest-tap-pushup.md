# Chest Tap Pushup

## Quick answer

The chest tap pushup is a challenging bodyweight exercise that targets the pectorals while also engaging the tricep and shoulders. It requires strength, coordination, and balance as you momentarily support your body with one hand while tapping your chest with the other. Advanced chest exercise using body weight. Targets chest, also works triceps, shoulders.

- **Body Part:** Chest
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Chest
- **Secondary Muscles:** Triceps, Shoulders
- **Difficulty:** Advanced
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Start in a high plank position with your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart and your body in a straight line.
2. Lower your body towards the ground by bending your elbows, keeping them close to your sides.
3. As you lower yourself, tap your chest with your right hand.
4. Push yourself back up to the starting position.
5. Repeat the movement, this time tapping your chest with your left hand.
6. Continue alternating sides for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Pushup explosively so your hands momentarily leave the ground, giving you time to tap your chest.
- Quickly return your hand to the ground before lowering into the next rep to maintain stability.
- Master regular Pushups for 20+ reps before attempting this plyometric variation.

## Common mistakes

- Not generating enough explosive force on the push, making it impossible to free a hand for the tap.
- Landing with locked elbows after the tap, which sends impact force through the elbow joint.
- Losing core tension during the tap, which causes the hips to sag and the body to twist.

## FAQ

### What is the purpose of the Chest Tap Pushup?

It builds upper body explosive power and unilateral stability. The brief moment on one hand forces the core and shoulder to stabilize under challenging conditions.

### Is the Chest Tap Pushup a plyometric exercise?

Yes, it is a plyometric Pushup variation that develops explosive chest and tricep power along with reactive stabilization ability.

### What should I master before Chest Tap Pushups?

You should be able to do 20+ regular Pushups and at least 10 explosive (clap or hand-lift) Pushups before attempting chest taps.

### What muscles does the Chest Tap Pushup work?

The Chest Tap Pushup primarily targets the chest, with secondary activation in the triceps, shoulders. It's classified as a strength exercise for the chest.

### Is the Chest Tap Pushup good for beginners?

The Chest Tap Pushup is an advanced exercise. Beginners should master fundamental chest movements first.
