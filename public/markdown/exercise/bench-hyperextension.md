# Bench Hyperextension - Forge Exercise Library

## Quick Answer

The bench hyperextension is a bodyweight exercise that targets the spinal erectors and lower back, while also engaging the glutes and hamstrings. It is performed on a hyperextension bench, requiring you to lower and raise your upper body while keeping the back straight.

**Details:**
- **Body Part:** Back
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Lower Back
- **Secondary Muscles:** Glutes, Hamstrings
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to Perform Bench Hyperextension

1. Adjust the hyperextension bench so that your hips are resting comfortably on the pad and your feet are secured.
2. Cross your arms over your chest or place your hands behind your head.
3. Slowly lower your upper body towards the ground while keeping your back straight.
4. Pause for a moment at the bottom, then raise your upper body back up until it is in line with your legs.
5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
