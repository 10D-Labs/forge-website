# Weighted Straight Bar Dip

## Quick answer

The weighted straight bar dip is a compound chest exercise performed on parallel bars with added weight for increased resistance. It primarily targets the pectorals, with significant involvement of the tricep and shoulders. Advanced chest exercise using body weight. Targets chest, also works triceps, shoulders.

- **Body Part:** Chest
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Chest
- **Secondary Muscles:** Triceps, Shoulders
- **Difficulty:** Advanced
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Position yourself between parallel bars with your arms fully extended and your body straight.
2. Lower your body by bending your elbows until your upper arms are parallel to the ground.
3. Pause for a moment, then push yourself back up to the starting position by straightening your arms.
4. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Lean forward slightly over the bar to shift emphasis onto the chest, or stay upright to hit triceps harder.
- Keep the bar in contact with your upper thighs or waist throughout the movement for control.
- Use a dip belt and start with 10-15 lbs, adding weight only when you can complete 5+ clean reps.

## Common mistakes

- Letting the weight swing during the movement, which destabilizes your position on the straight bar.
- Going too deep and letting the shoulders roll forward past the bar, which stresses the shoulder joint.
- Gripping the bar too tightly with locked wrists, causing forearm cramping and limiting rep performance.

## FAQ

### What's the difference between straight bar dips and parallel bar dips?

Straight bar dips are performed on a single bar, which limits your range of motion and shifts more work to the triceps and front delts. Parallel bar dips allow deeper range and more chest involvement.

### Are Weighted Straight Bar Dips good for muscle building?

Yes, they build impressive triceps and front delt strength. The straight bar variation emphasizes lockout strength more than parallel bar dips.

### How do I keep the weight from swinging?

Use a shorter chain on your dip belt, squeeze the weight between your thighs, or use a weight vest instead. Controlled reps also reduce swing.

### What muscles does the Weighted Straight Bar Dip work?

The Weighted Straight Bar Dip primarily targets the chest, with secondary activation in the triceps, shoulders. It's classified as a strength exercise for the chest.

### Is the Weighted Straight Bar Dip good for beginners?

The Weighted Straight Bar Dip is an advanced exercise. Beginners should master fundamental chest movements first.
