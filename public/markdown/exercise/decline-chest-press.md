# Decline Chest Press

## Quick answer

The decline chest press is a machine-based exercise targeting the pectoral muscles, with secondary emphasis on the tricep and shoulders. It is performed on a machine, providing guided movement for safety and stability. Beginner chest exercise using machine. Targets chest, also works triceps, shoulders.

- **Body Part:** Chest
- **Equipment:** Machine
- **Target Muscle:** Chest
- **Secondary Muscles:** Triceps, Shoulders
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Adjust the seat height and backrest of the machine to a comfortable position.
2. Sit on the machine with your back against the backrest and your feet flat on the floor.
3. Grasp the handles with an overhand grip and position your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
4. Push the handles forward and away from your body until your arms are fully extended.
5. Slowly lower the handles back towards your chest, keeping your elbows slightly bent.
6. Pause for a moment at the bottom, then push the handles back to the starting position.
7. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Set the seat height so the handles are aligned with your lower chest or just below your nipple line.
- Push through the lower part of your palm and focus on squeezing your lower pecs at full extension.
- Control the return phase for a full 2-3 seconds to maximize time under tension on the lower chest.

## Common mistakes

- Letting the shoulders roll forward at the top of the press instead of keeping the chest proud.
- Setting the seat too high, which turns the decline press into essentially a flat press angle.
- Slamming the weight stack at the bottom by releasing the handles too fast on the eccentric.

## FAQ

### What part of the chest does the decline press target?

The decline angle emphasizes the lower (sternal) fibers of the pectoralis major, giving the chest a fuller, more defined look at the bottom.

### Should I do decline press before or after flat press?

If your lower chest is a weak point, do it first. Otherwise, perform it after flat pressing as an accessory to round out overall chest development.

### Is the machine decline press safer than barbell decline?

Yes, the fixed path eliminates the risk of dropping a barbell on your face or chest, and you can safely push to failure without a spotter.

### What muscles does the Decline Chest Press work?

The Decline Chest Press primarily targets the chest, with secondary activation in the triceps, shoulders. It's classified as a strength exercise for the chest.

### Is the Decline Chest Press good for beginners?

Yes, the Decline Chest Press is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building chest strength.
