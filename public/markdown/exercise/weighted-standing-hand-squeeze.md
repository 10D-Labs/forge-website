# Weighted Standing Hand Squeeze

## Quick answer

The weighted standing hand squeeze is an exercise targeting the forearms, performed by squeezing weights in each hand while standing. This movement helps to improve grip strength and forearm endurance. Beginner arms exercise using body weight. Targets forearms, also works biceps, triceps.

- **Body Part:** Arms
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Forearms
- **Secondary Muscles:** Biceps, Triceps
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a weight in each hand.
2. Extend your arms straight in front of you, palms facing each other.
3. Squeeze your hands together as hard as you can, engaging your forearms.
4. Hold the squeeze for a few seconds, then release.
5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Squeeze as hard as possible for 3-5 seconds per rep rather than doing quick, incomplete squeezes.
- Keep your wrists in a neutral position to target the forearm flexors evenly.
- Use a gripper, stress ball, or thick-handled dumbbell for varied resistance and angles.

## Common mistakes

- Rushing through reps without holding the contraction long enough to fatigue the forearm muscles.
- Bending the wrists during the squeeze, which can strain the wrist tendons.
- Only training grip in one plane when forearm strength requires multiple angles.

## FAQ

### How often should you train grip strength?

2-3 times per week is sufficient. Grip muscles recover relatively quickly but can be overtrained if done daily with high volume.

### Does grip training help with Deadlifts?

Absolutely. Stronger grip directly translates to heavier Deadlifts, rows, and Pullups without needing straps.

### What are the best exercises to pair with hand squeezes?

Wrist curls, reverse wrist curls, farmer carries, and plate pinches together provide comprehensive forearm and grip development.

### What muscles does the Weighted Standing Hand Squeeze work?

The Weighted Standing Hand Squeeze primarily targets the forearms, with secondary activation in the biceps, triceps. It's classified as a strength exercise for the arms.

### Is the Weighted Standing Hand Squeeze good for beginners?

Yes, the Weighted Standing Hand Squeeze is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building forearms strength.
