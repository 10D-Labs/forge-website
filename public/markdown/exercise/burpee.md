# Burpee

## Quick answer

The burpee is a full-body exercise that combines a squat, pushup, and jump to elevate heart rate and build strength and endurance. Intermediate cardio exercise using body weight. Targets cardiovascular, also works quadriceps, hamstrings, calves, shoulders, chest.

- **Body Part:** Cardio
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Cardiovascular
- **Secondary Muscles:** Quadriceps, Hamstrings, Calves, Shoulders, Chest
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Cardio

---

## How to perform

1. Start in a standing position with your feet shoulder-width apart.
2. Lower your body into a squat position by bending your knees and placing your hands on the floor in front of you.
3. Kick your feet back into a pushup position.
4. Perform a pushup, keeping your body in a straight line.
5. Jump your feet back into the squat position.
6. Jump up explosively, reaching your arms overhead.
7. Land softly and immediately lower back into a squat position to begin the next repetition.

## Pro tips

- Land softly from the jump by bending your knees to absorb the impact and immediately flow into the next squat.
- Keep your core tight during the Pushup portion to prevent your hips from sagging.
- Breathe out during the jump and in during the descent to maintain a consistent breathing pattern.

## Common mistakes

- Skipping the Pushup or doing a partial Pushup to save energy, which reduces the upper body training effect.
- Landing with straight legs after the jump, which sends shock through the knees and spine.
- Letting the hips sag during the Pushup position, which strains the lower back.

## FAQ

### How many calories do Burpees burn?

Burpees burn roughly 10-15 calories per minute depending on intensity and body weight. They are one of the most efficient calorie-burning bodyweight exercises.

### Are Burpees bad for your knees?

Not inherently, but poor landing mechanics can stress the knees. Land softly with bent knees and avoid jumping on hard surfaces. Skip the jump if you have knee issues.

### What is a good number of Burpees per minute?

Beginners aim for 6-8 per minute. Intermediate trainees target 10-12. Advanced athletes can sustain 15+ per minute with good form.

### What muscles does the Burpee work?

The Burpee primarily targets the cardiovascular, with secondary activation in the quadriceps, hamstrings, calves, shoulders, chest. It's classified as a cardio exercise for the cardio.

### Is the Burpee good for beginners?

The Burpee is rated as intermediate. Beginners should build a base of cardiovascular strength with easier variations first.
