# Dumbbell Seated Inner Bicep Curl - Forge Exercise Library

## Quick Answer

The dumbbell seated inner bicep curl targets the bicep and forearms by having the lifter sit on a bench, rest their upper arms on their thighs, and curl dumbbells upward. This isolates the bicep and minimizes momentum, making it an effective strength-building exercise.

**Details:**
- **Body Part:** Arms
- **Equipment:** Dumbbell
- **Target Muscle:** Biceps
- **Secondary Muscles:** Forearms
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to Perform Dumbbell Seated Inner Bicep Curl

1. Sit on a bench with your feet flat on the ground and hold a dumbbell in each hand, palms facing up.
2. Rest your upper arms on your thighs, allowing the dumbbells to hang down.
3. Keeping your upper arms stationary, curl the dumbbells up towards your shoulders by contracting your biceps.
4. Pause for a moment at the top, then slowly lower the dumbbells back down to the starting position.
5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
