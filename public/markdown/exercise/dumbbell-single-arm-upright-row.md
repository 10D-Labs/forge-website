# Dumbbell Single Arm Upright Row

## Quick answer

The dumbbell one arm upright row is a unilateral shoulder exercise that targets the deltoids, with secondary emphasis on the traps and bicep. It involves lifting a dumbbell with one arm in an upright rowing motion. Beginner shoulders exercise using dumbbell. Targets shoulders, also works traps, biceps.

- **Body Part:** Shoulders
- **Equipment:** Dumbbell
- **Target Muscle:** Shoulders
- **Secondary Muscles:** Traps, Biceps
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in one hand with an overhand grip.
2. Let the dumbbell hang at arm's length in front of your thighs, with your palm facing your body.
3. Keeping your back straight and your core engaged, exhale and lift the dumbbell straight up towards your chin, leading with your elbow.
4. Pause for a moment at the top, then inhale and slowly lower the dumbbell back down to the starting position.
5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, then switch to the other arm.

## Pro tips

- Lead with the elbow and pull the dumbbell straight up along your body to shoulder height for maximum delt and trap engagement.
- Keep the dumbbell close to your body throughout the pull rather than letting it drift forward.
- Use your free hand on your hip or a support to prevent body lean and keep the movement strict.

## Common mistakes

- Pulling the dumbbell too high above shoulder level, which can impinge the shoulder joint.
- Leaning to the opposite side to compensate for the one-sided load instead of staying upright.
- Using body momentum to swing the weight up rather than pulling with a controlled shoulder and trap contraction.

## FAQ

### Are upright rows bad for the shoulders?

They can be if performed with excessive height or internal rotation. Keep the pull to shoulder height or slightly below and use a single dumbbell for better joint freedom than a barbell.

### What muscles does the single arm upright row work?

Primarily the lateral delts and upper traps, with secondary work from the biceps and forearms.

### Is the single arm version safer than the Barbell Upright Row?

Generally yes. The dumbbell allows your wrist and shoulder to rotate freely, reducing the impingement risk that a fixed barbell grip can cause.

### What muscles does the Dumbbell Single Arm Upright Row work?

The Dumbbell Single Arm Upright Row primarily targets the shoulders, with secondary activation in the traps, biceps. It's classified as a strength exercise for the shoulders.

### Is the Dumbbell Single Arm Upright Row good for beginners?

Yes, the Dumbbell Single Arm Upright Row is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building shoulders strength.
