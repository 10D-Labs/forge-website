# Stairmaster (Time)

## Quick answer

The stairmaster is a cardiovascular exercise performed on a machine that simulates stair climbing. It primarily targets the cardiovascular system while also engaging the lower body muscles. Beginner cardio exercise using cardio machine. Targets cardiovascular, also works quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, calves.

- **Body Part:** Cardio
- **Equipment:** Cardio Machine
- **Target Muscle:** Cardiovascular
- **Secondary Muscles:** Quadriceps, Hamstrings, Glutes, Calves
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Cardio

---

## How to perform

1. Adjust the machine to a comfortable level.
2. Step onto the machine and place your hands on the handrails for support.
3. Start walking by placing one foot on a step and then the other, alternating between legs.
4. Maintain an upright posture and engage your core muscles.
5. Continue climbing for your target duration.
6. Gradually decrease the intensity to cool down.
7. Remember to stretch after completing the exercise.

## Pro tips

- Stand upright and only lightly touch the handrails for balance rather than gripping and supporting your weight.
- Drive through the full foot on each step rather than just the toes to engage your glutes and hamstrings.
- Start at a moderate pace and increase speed after 3-5 minutes once your muscles are warmed up.

## Common mistakes

- Leaning on the handrails and supporting body weight with the arms, which drastically reduces calorie burn and leg engagement.
- Taking very shallow steps on the toes only, which overloads the calves and underworks the glutes.
- Hunching forward at the waist, which strains the lower back and reduces core engagement.

## FAQ

### Is the StairMaster good for losing belly fat?

The StairMaster is great for overall fat loss through calorie expenditure. No exercise targets belly fat specifically, but consistent StairMaster work combined with a calorie deficit will reduce body fat.

### How long should I use the StairMaster?

For cardiovascular health and fat loss, 20-40 minutes at a moderate intensity is effective. Start with 15 minutes if you are new to it.

### Does the StairMaster build glutes?

It provides moderate glute activation, especially when you drive through the whole foot and skip steps. For significant glute growth, combine it with resistance training exercises like squats and hip thrusts.

### What muscles does the Stairmaster (Time) work?

The Stairmaster (Time) primarily targets the cardiovascular, with secondary activation in the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, calves. It's classified as a cardio exercise for the cardio.

### Is the Stairmaster (Time) good for beginners?

Yes, the Stairmaster (Time) is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building cardiovascular strength.
