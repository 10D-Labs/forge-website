# Short Stride Run (Distance)

## Quick answer

Short stride run is a cardiovascular exercise performed by running with quick, short steps. It primarily targets the cardiovascular system and also engages the quadriceps, hamstrings, and calves. The shorter stride length increases cadence and can help improve running efficiency. Beginner cardio exercise using body weight. Targets cardiovascular, also works quadriceps, hamstrings, calves.

- **Body Part:** Cardio
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Cardiovascular
- **Secondary Muscles:** Quadriceps, Hamstrings, Calves
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Cardio

---

## How to perform

1. Find an open space to perform the exercise.
2. Stand tall with your feet hip-width apart.
3. Start running with quick, short steps, maintaining rapid leg turnover.
4. Keep your feet close to the ground and avoid overstriding.
5. Continue running with short strides until you reach your target distance.

## Pro tips

- Focus on high leg turnover rate rather than covering ground with each step.
- Keep your feet close to the ground with a quick, light cadence of 180+ steps per minute.
- Maintain an upright posture with a slight forward lean from the ankles, not the waist.

## Common mistakes

- Unconsciously increasing stride length as fatigue sets in, losing the short-stride benefit.
- Shuffling the feet without adequate knee lift, which reduces efficiency and increases tripping risk.
- Tensing the upper body, especially the shoulders and hands, which wastes energy.

## FAQ

### When should I use short stride running?

It's useful for active recovery runs, running drills, and for runners working on cadence improvement. It's also gentler on joints for longer distance efforts.

### Does short stride running burn fewer calories?

Not necessarily. While each stride is shorter, the higher cadence means you take more steps per minute. The calorie burn is similar to normal-stride running at comparable effort.

### Can short stride running prevent injuries?

Research suggests higher cadence with shorter strides reduces impact forces per step, which can decrease the risk of common running injuries like shin splints and runner's knee.

### What muscles does the Short Stride Run (Distance) work?

The Short Stride Run (Distance) primarily targets the cardiovascular, with secondary activation in the quadriceps, hamstrings, calves. It's classified as a cardio exercise for the cardio.

### Is the Short Stride Run (Distance) good for beginners?

Yes, the Short Stride Run (Distance) is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building cardiovascular strength.
