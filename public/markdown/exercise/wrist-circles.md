# Wrist Circles

## Quick answer

Wrist circles are a bodyweight exercise focused on improving mobility and flexibility in the wrists and forearms. This movement is commonly used as a warm-up or rehabilitation exercise to increase blood flow and prepare the wrists for more strenuous activities. Beginner arms exercise using body weight. Targets forearms, also works hands, wrists.

- **Body Part:** Arms
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Forearms
- **Secondary Muscles:** Hands, Wrists
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Mobility

---

## How to perform

1. Extend your arms straight out in front of you.
2. Make a fist with both hands.
3. Rotate your wrists in a circular motion, keeping your arms still.
4. Continue the wrist circles for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Make the circles as large as possible to move the wrist through its full range in all directions.
- Perform 10-15 circles in each direction per hand, moving slowly enough to feel any stiff spots.
- Keep your forearms still and isolate the movement entirely at the wrist joint.

## Common mistakes

- Making tiny, fast circles that do not challenge the full range of motion.
- Moving the entire forearm and elbow instead of isolating the rotation at the wrist.
- Skipping this warm-up before exercises that load the wrists (Pushups, bench press, front squats).

## FAQ

### When should I do Wrist Circles?

Before any exercise that loads the wrists, such as Pushups, bench press, front squats, cleans, or Handstands. Also beneficial for desk workers to prevent repetitive strain.

### Can Wrist Circles help with wrist pain?

They can help with stiffness and mild discomfort by increasing blood flow and synovial fluid production in the joint. For persistent pain, see a healthcare professional.

### How long does a proper wrist warm-up take?

About 30-60 seconds total. Ten circles each direction per hand is sufficient for most people.

### What muscles does the Wrist Circles work?

The Wrist Circles primarily targets the forearms, with secondary activation in the hands, wrists. It's classified as a mobility exercise for the arms.

### Is the Wrist Circles good for beginners?

Yes, the Wrist Circles is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building forearms strength.
