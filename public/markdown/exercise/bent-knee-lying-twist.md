# Bent Knee Lying Twist - Forge Exercise Library

## Quick Answer

The bent knee lying twist is a bodyweight exercise that targets the glutes and also engages the obliques and hip flexors. It involves lying on your back with bent knees and twisting them side to side, which helps improve flexibility and mobility in the lower back and hips.

**Details:**
- **Body Part:** Legs
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Glutes
- **Secondary Muscles:** Obliques, Hip Flexors
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Mobility

---

## How to Perform Bent Knee Lying Twist

1. Lie flat on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the ground.
2. Extend your arms out to the sides, perpendicular to your body.
3. Keeping your knees together, slowly lower them to one side, aiming to touch the ground with your knees.
4. Pause for a moment, then engage your core and slowly lift your knees back to the starting position.
5. Repeat the movement to the other side.
6. Continue alternating sides for the desired number of repetitions.
