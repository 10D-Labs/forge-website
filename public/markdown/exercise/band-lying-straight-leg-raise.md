# Band Lying Straight Leg Raise

## Quick answer

The band lying straight leg raise is an abdominal exercise that also engages the hip flexors. It uses a resistance band to add difficulty and helps strengthen the core and lower body. Intermediate core exercise using resistance band. Targets abs, also works hip flexors.

- **Body Part:** Core
- **Equipment:** Resistance Band
- **Target Muscle:** Abs
- **Secondary Muscles:** Hip Flexors
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Lie flat on your back with your legs straight and your feet together.
2. Place the band around the arches of your feet and hold the ends of the band with your hands.
3. Engaging your abs, lift both legs off the ground until they are perpendicular to the floor.
4. Pause for a moment at the top, then slowly lower your legs back down to the starting position.
5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Keep your legs fully straight and raise them to 90 degrees while pressing your lower back flat against the floor.
- Lower your legs as slowly as possible, stopping before your lower back starts to arch off the ground.
- Hold the band ends firmly with your hands at your sides to add tension against the leg raise.

## Common mistakes

- Allowing the lower back to arch off the floor during the lowering phase, which strains the lumbar spine.
- Bending the knees as the legs descend due to insufficient hamstring flexibility or ab strength.
- Lowering the legs all the way to the floor when the abs cannot maintain a flat back position.

## FAQ

### Are straight leg raises bad for your back?

Only if your lower back arches off the floor. Keep the back flat by only lowering your legs as far as you can while maintaining floor contact with the lumbar spine.

### What does the band add to a straight leg raise?

The band adds resistance to the leg raise portion, making the abs and hip flexors work harder throughout the full range of motion.

### How do you know if you are lowering your legs too far?

If you feel your lower back lift off the floor, you have gone too far. Stop at that point and only work within the range where your back stays flat.

### What muscles does the Band Lying Straight Leg Raise work?

The Band Lying Straight Leg Raise primarily targets the abs, with secondary activation in the hip flexors. It's classified as a strength exercise for the core.

### Is the Band Lying Straight Leg Raise good for beginners?

The Band Lying Straight Leg Raise is rated as intermediate. Beginners should build a base of abs strength with easier variations first.
