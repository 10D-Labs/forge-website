# Lying Glutes Stretch

## Quick answer

The assisted lying glutes stretch is a flexibility exercise targeting the gluteal muscles. It is performed lying on your back, crossing one ankle over the opposite thigh, and gently pulling the thigh towards your chest to stretch the glutes and hamstrings. Beginner legs exercise using machine. Targets glutes, also works hamstrings.

- **Body Part:** Legs
- **Equipment:** Machine
- **Target Muscle:** Glutes
- **Secondary Muscles:** Hamstrings
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Stretching

---

## How to perform

1. Lie on your back with your legs extended.
2. Bend your right knee and place your right ankle on your left thigh, just above the knee.
3. Grasp your left thigh with both hands and gently pull it towards your chest.
4. Hold the stretch for 20-30 seconds.
5. Release and repeat on the other side.

## Pro tips

- Place your ankle on the opposite thigh just above the knee, not on the kneecap itself.
- Pull your bottom thigh toward your chest while gently pressing the top knee away from you to deepen the stretch.
- Keep your head and shoulders relaxed on the floor to avoid creating tension in the neck.

## Common mistakes

- Placing the ankle on the kneecap, which can cause knee discomfort.
- Lifting the head and shoulders off the ground, which creates unnecessary neck tension.
- Not pulling the bottom thigh close enough to the chest, which limits the depth of the glute stretch.

## FAQ

### What does the Lying Glutes Stretch target?

It primarily stretches the gluteus maximus and medius of the crossed leg, with secondary stretching of the piriformis and external hip rotators.

### How long should I hold this stretch?

Hold for 30-60 seconds per side. For deeper flexibility work or to relieve tightness, hold for up to 2 minutes per side.

### Can this stretch help with lower back pain?

Yes, tight glutes pull on the pelvis and can contribute to lower back pain. Regular stretching helps maintain proper pelvic alignment and reduces lumbar strain.

### What muscles does the Lying Glutes Stretch work?

The Lying Glutes Stretch primarily targets the glutes, with secondary activation in the hamstrings. It's classified as a stretching exercise for the legs.

### Is the Lying Glutes Stretch good for beginners?

Yes, the Lying Glutes Stretch is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building glutes strength.
