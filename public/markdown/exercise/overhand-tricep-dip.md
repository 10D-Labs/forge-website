# Overhand Tricep Dip

## Quick answer

The overhand tricep dip is a machine-based exercise targeting the tricep, with secondary emphasis on the chest and shoulders. It involves lowering and raising the body using a machine, which provides stability and controlled resistance. Beginner arms exercise using machine. Targets triceps, also works chest, shoulders.

- **Body Part:** Arms
- **Equipment:** Machine
- **Target Muscle:** Triceps
- **Secondary Muscles:** Chest, Shoulders
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Adjust the machine to the appropriate height and secure your body in position.
2. Grasp the handles with an overhand grip and position your body so that your arms are fully extended.
3. Lower your body by bending your elbows, keeping your upper arms close to your sides.
4. Continue lowering until your upper arms are parallel to the floor.
5. Pause for a moment, then push yourself back up to the starting position by extending your elbows.
6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Keep your torso upright throughout the dip to emphasize the triceps. Leaning forward shifts work to the chest.
- Lower yourself until your upper arms are parallel to the floor, then press back up to full extension.
- Lock out fully at the top of each rep and squeeze the triceps hard.

## Common mistakes

- Going too deep past parallel, which puts excessive stress on the shoulder joint.
- Leaning forward to use the chest muscles instead of staying upright for triceps isolation.
- Using momentum by bouncing at the bottom instead of pressing with controlled force.

## FAQ

### How is an Overhand Tricep Dip different from a regular dip?

The overhand grip and upright torso position shift emphasis to the triceps rather than the chest. The machine also provides assistance or resistance, making it more accessible than bodyweight dips.

### Can machine dips replace bodyweight dips?

For muscle building, yes. Machine dips provide consistent resistance and are easier to progressively overload. Bodyweight dips are better for functional strength and stabilizer development.

### What muscles does the Overhand Tricep Dip work?

The Overhand Tricep Dip primarily targets the triceps, with secondary activation in the chest, shoulders. It's classified as a strength exercise for the arms.

### Is the Overhand Tricep Dip good for beginners?

Yes, the Overhand Tricep Dip is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building triceps strength.
