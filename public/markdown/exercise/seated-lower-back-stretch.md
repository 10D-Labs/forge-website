# Seated Lower Back Stretch

## Quick answer

A gentle stretch performed while seated to target the lower back and lats, also engaging the glutes and hamstrings. This exercise is suitable for improving flexibility and relieving tension in the lower back area. Beginner back exercise using body weight. Targets lats, also works glutes, hamstrings.

- **Body Part:** Back
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Lats
- **Secondary Muscles:** Glutes, Hamstrings
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Stretching

---

## How to perform

1. Sit on the edge of a chair with your feet flat on the ground.
2. Place your hands on your thighs or on the sides of the chair for support.
3. Slowly lean forward from your hips, keeping your back straight.
4. Feel the stretch in your lower back and hold for 20-30 seconds.
5. Slowly return to the starting position and repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Hinge forward from the hips rather than rounding the spine to stretch the lower back safely.
- Let your head and neck relax as you lean forward; don't crane your neck to look up.
- Breathe deeply into the stretch and let gravity pull your torso forward gradually.

## Common mistakes

- Aggressively rounding the spine to 'push' into the stretch instead of gently hinging at the hips.
- Bouncing in and out of the stretch, which can irritate the lower back muscles and ligaments.
- Holding the breath, which causes the muscles to tense up rather than relax.

## FAQ

### How often should I do the Seated Lower Back Stretch?

Daily stretching is ideal if you sit for long periods. Hold for 20-30 seconds and repeat 2-3 times. It's especially beneficial after prolonged sitting.

### Is it safe to stretch my lower back if it hurts?

Gentle stretching can relieve muscle tightness, but avoid stretching through sharp or shooting pain. If the pain persists, see a healthcare professional before continuing.

### Should I do this stretch before or after a workout?

After a workout when the muscles are warm. Pre-workout, use dynamic movements like cat-cow stretches instead of static holds for the lower back.

### What muscles does the Seated Lower Back Stretch work?

The Seated Lower Back Stretch primarily targets the lats, with secondary activation in the glutes, hamstrings. It's classified as a stretching exercise for the back.

### Is the Seated Lower Back Stretch good for beginners?

Yes, the Seated Lower Back Stretch is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building lats strength.
