# Reverse Hyper on Flat Bench

## Quick answer

The reverse hyper on flat bench is a bodyweight exercise targeting the glutes, with secondary emphasis on the hamstrings and lower back. It involves lying face down on a flat bench and lifting the legs upward, focusing on squeezing the glutes at the top of the movement. Beginner legs exercise using body weight. Targets glutes, also works hamstrings, lower back.

- **Body Part:** Legs
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Glutes
- **Secondary Muscles:** Hamstrings, Lower Back
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Lie face down on a flat bench with your hips at the edge and your legs hanging off the bench.
2. Hold onto the bench for stability.
3. Keeping your legs straight, raise them up towards the ceiling as high as you can.
4. Squeeze your glutes at the top of the movement.
5. Slowly lower your legs back down to the starting position.
6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Grip the edges of the bench firmly and keep your upper body completely still as you raise and lower your legs.
- Lift your legs until they are roughly parallel with the floor, not higher, to avoid hyperextending the lower back.
- Squeeze your glutes hard at the top of each rep and lower slowly for 2-3 seconds.

## Common mistakes

- Swinging the legs up using momentum instead of controlled glute and hamstring contraction.
- Lifting the legs too high past parallel, which hyperextends the lumbar spine and can cause pain.
- Sliding forward on the bench during the set because the hips are not properly positioned at the edge.

## FAQ

### Is a flat bench reverse hyper as effective as a reverse hyper machine?

It is effective for lighter loads and bodyweight. A dedicated machine allows loading weight, which makes it superior for progressive overload. The bench version is a great accessible alternative.

### What muscles does the reverse hyper work?

Primarily the glutes and hamstrings, with significant lower back (erector spinae) involvement. It also gently decompresses the spine during the eccentric phase.

### Can I add weight to this exercise?

Yes. Hold a light dumbbell between your feet or use ankle weights. Start very light since the leverage is long and even small weights feel heavy.

### What muscles does the Reverse Hyper on Flat Bench work?

The Reverse Hyper on Flat Bench primarily targets the glutes, with secondary activation in the hamstrings, lower back. It's classified as a strength exercise for the legs.

### Is the Reverse Hyper on Flat Bench good for beginners?

Yes, the Reverse Hyper on Flat Bench is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building glutes strength.
