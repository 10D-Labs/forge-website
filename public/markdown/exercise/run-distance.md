# Run (Distance)

## Quick answer

Running is a fundamental cardiovascular exercise that can be performed outdoors or on a track. It builds endurance, burns calories, and strengthens the lower body muscles while improving cardiovascular health. Beginner cardio exercise using body weight. Targets cardiovascular, also works quadriceps, hamstrings, calves, glutes.

- **Body Part:** Cardio
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Cardiovascular
- **Secondary Muscles:** Quadriceps, Hamstrings, Calves, Glutes
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Cardio

---

## How to perform

1. Begin with a brief warm-up walk or light jog.
2. Start running at a comfortable pace, landing with a midfoot strike.
3. Keep your arms bent at 90 degrees, swinging them naturally with each stride.
4. Maintain an upright posture with your head up and shoulders relaxed.
5. Breathe rhythmically, inhaling through your nose and exhaling through your mouth.
6. Continue running until you reach your target distance.
7. Finish with a cool-down walk and stretching.

## Pro tips

- Start slower than you think you should; the goal is to finish the distance feeling strong, not to burn out early.
- Land with a midfoot strike under your hips, not with your heel reaching out in front of you.
- Aim for a cadence of 170-180 steps per minute to reduce impact and improve efficiency.

## Common mistakes

- Starting too fast and hitting a wall halfway through the distance, resulting in a slower overall time.
- Overstriding (reaching the foot out in front), which acts as a brake with every step and increases injury risk.
- Running with tense shoulders hunched up by the ears, which wastes energy and restricts breathing.

## FAQ

### How do I build up my running distance?

Follow the 10% rule: increase weekly distance by no more than 10%. If you currently run 10 miles per week, add no more than 1 mile the following week.

### How fast should I run for distance?

Conversational pace for most distance runs. If you can't hold a conversation, slow down. About 80% of your running should be at easy, conversational effort.

### Should I run every day?

Most people benefit from 3-4 running days per week with rest or cross-training days in between. Running daily increases overuse injury risk, especially for newer runners.

### What muscles does the Run (Distance) work?

The Run (Distance) primarily targets the cardiovascular, with secondary activation in the quadriceps, hamstrings, calves, glutes. It's classified as a cardio exercise for the cardio.

### Is the Run (Distance) good for beginners?

Yes, the Run (Distance) is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building cardiovascular strength.
