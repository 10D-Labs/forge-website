# Band Single Arm Single Leg Split Squat

## Quick answer

The band one arm single leg split squat is a lower body exercise that targets the quadriceps, with secondary emphasis on the glutes and hamstrings. It involves performing a split squat on one leg with the rear foot elevated and a resistance band around the ankles, challenging balance and unilateral strength. Intermediate legs exercise using resistance band. Targets quads, also works glutes, hamstrings.

- **Body Part:** Legs
- **Equipment:** Resistance Band
- **Target Muscle:** Quads
- **Secondary Muscles:** Glutes, Hamstrings
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Stand with your feet hip-width apart and place a resistance band around your ankles.
2. Extend one leg forward and rest the top of your foot on a bench or step behind you.
3. Hold onto a support with one hand for balance.
4. Bend your standing leg and lower your body down into a squat position, keeping your knee in line with your toes.
5. Push through your heel to return to the starting position.
6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, then switch legs.

## Pro tips

- Hold the band in one hand at shoulder height and use the opposite leg as the front leg for a contralateral loading pattern.
- Keep your front knee tracking over your toes and do not let it collapse inward.
- Lower until your back knee nearly touches the ground for a full range of motion.

## Common mistakes

- Leaning too far forward over the front leg instead of keeping the torso upright.
- Not stepping far enough apart, which limits the depth and range of motion of the split squat.
- Losing balance because the single-arm band hold creates an asymmetric load that was not anticipated.

## FAQ

### Why hold the band in one arm during a split squat?

The asymmetric load forces the core to work harder to resist lateral flexion and rotation, making it a full-body functional exercise.

### What band thickness is appropriate?

Start with a medium band. The split squat itself is challenging, and adding asymmetric resistance should not compromise your form.

### How is this different from a regular Bulgarian split squat?

The band adds variable resistance and the single-arm hold creates an anti-rotation demand. The rear foot may or may not be elevated depending on the variation.

### What muscles does the Band Single Arm Single Leg Split Squat work?

The Band Single Arm Single Leg Split Squat primarily targets the quads, with secondary activation in the glutes, hamstrings. It's classified as a strength exercise for the legs.

### Is the Band Single Arm Single Leg Split Squat good for beginners?

The Band Single Arm Single Leg Split Squat is rated as intermediate. Beginners should build a base of quads strength with easier variations first.
