# Dumbbell Single Arm Incline Chest Press - Forge Exercise Library

## Quick Answer

The dumbbell one arm incline chest press is a unilateral upper body exercise that targets the pectoral muscles, with secondary emphasis on the shoulders and tricep. It is performed on an incline bench using a single dumbbell, which increases the demand for core stability and coordination.

**Details:**
- **Body Part:** Chest
- **Equipment:** Dumbbell
- **Target Muscle:** Chest
- **Secondary Muscles:** Shoulders, Triceps
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to Perform Dumbbell Single Arm Incline Chest Press

1. Adjust the incline bench to a 45 degree angle.
2. Sit on the bench with your back against the pad and feet flat on the ground.
3. Hold a dumbbell in one hand with an overhand grip, resting it on your shoulder.
4. Push the dumbbell up and away from your body, extending your arm fully.
5. Pause for a moment at the top, then slowly lower the dumbbell back down to the starting position.
6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, then switch to the other arm.
