# Hands Bike (Distance)

## Quick answer

The hands bike is a cardiovascular exercise that targets the pectorals, triceps, and shoulders by requiring the user to pedal with their hands, simulating a cycling motion for the upper body. Beginner cardio exercise using cardio machine. Targets cardiovascular, also works triceps, shoulders.

- **Body Part:** Cardio
- **Equipment:** Cardio Machine
- **Target Muscle:** Cardiovascular
- **Secondary Muscles:** Triceps, Shoulders
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Cardio

---

## How to perform

1. Adjust the seat height and handlebar position to a comfortable level.
2. Sit on the ergometer with your back straight and feet on the pedals.
3. Grasp the handles with your hands and position your arms at a 90 degree angle.
4. Start pedaling with your hands, pushing and pulling the handles in a controlled motion.
5. Continue pedaling until you reach your target distance.

## Pro tips

- Sit tall with your back straight and engage your core throughout each pedaling cycle.
- Push and pull with equal effort on each arm to maintain a smooth, even pedaling rhythm.
- Keep your shoulders down and relaxed; don't shrug them up toward your ears as you fatigue.

## Common mistakes

- Rounding the back and slumping forward, which reduces breathing capacity and can strain the lower back.
- Using only a pushing motion instead of a full push-pull cycle, which limits the muscles being worked.
- Setting the resistance too low, making the movement feel easy but providing minimal training stimulus.

## FAQ

### What muscles does the hand bike work?

Primarily the shoulders, triceps (pushing phase), and biceps (pulling phase). The core also works to stabilize the torso. It's one of the few cardio options that focuses on the upper body.

### Who benefits most from the hand bike?

It's especially useful for people with lower body injuries who want to maintain cardiovascular fitness. It's also popular for active recovery and warming up before upper body workouts.

### What resistance and pace should I use?

For steady cardio, use moderate resistance at 60-80 RPM. For intervals, increase resistance and push to 90+ RPM during work periods.

### What muscles does the Hands Bike (Distance) work?

The Hands Bike (Distance) primarily targets the cardiovascular, with secondary activation in the triceps, shoulders. It's classified as a cardio exercise for the cardio.

### Is the Hands Bike (Distance) good for beginners?

Yes, the Hands Bike (Distance) is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building cardiovascular strength.
