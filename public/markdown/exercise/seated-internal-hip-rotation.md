# Seated Internal Hip Rotation - Forge Exercise Library

## Quick Answer

The seated internal hip rotation is an exercise that targets the glutes and helps improve hip mobility and strength. It is performed while seated, using a resistance band to provide external resistance as you rotate your knees outward.

**Details:**
- **Body Part:** Legs
- **Equipment:** Resistance Band
- **Target Muscle:** Glutes
- **Secondary Muscles:** Hamstrings, Quadriceps
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Mobility

---

## How to Perform Seated Internal Hip Rotation

1. Sit on a chair or bench with your back straight and feet flat on the ground.
2. Place a resistance band around your thighs, just above your knees.
3. Keep your knees bent at a 90 degree angle and your feet shoulder-width apart.
4. Engage your glutes and slowly rotate your knees outward, pushing against the resistance of the band.
5. Pause for a moment at the end of the movement, then slowly return to the starting position.
6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
