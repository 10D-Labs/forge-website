# Treadmill Run (Time)

## Quick answer

Treadmill running is a cardiovascular exercise performed on a motorized treadmill machine. It allows for controlled pacing, incline adjustments, and weather-independent training, making it ideal for consistent cardio workouts. Beginner cardio exercise using treadmill. Targets cardiovascular, also works quadriceps, hamstrings, calves, glutes.

- **Body Part:** Cardio
- **Equipment:** Treadmill
- **Target Muscle:** Cardiovascular
- **Secondary Muscles:** Quadriceps, Hamstrings, Calves, Glutes
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Cardio

---

## How to perform

1. Step onto the treadmill and straddle the belt, placing your feet on the side rails.
2. Attach the safety clip to your clothing if available.
3. Start the treadmill at a slow walking pace to warm up.
4. Gradually increase the speed to your desired running pace.
5. Maintain an upright posture with your arms swinging naturally at your sides.
6. Land with a midfoot strike and push off with your toes.
7. Continue running for your target duration.
8. Gradually decrease speed to cool down, then carefully step off the treadmill.

## Pro tips

- Set a 1-2% incline to better simulate outdoor running conditions and reduce the impact of the belt assisting your stride.
- Avoid holding the handrails while running; use them only for balance during speed changes.
- Match your cadence to about 170-180 steps per minute for efficient running form.

## Common mistakes

- Overstriding (landing with the foot far in front of the body), which is amplified on a treadmill and causes braking forces on each step.
- Holding onto the handrails while running, which alters your natural gait and reduces caloric expenditure.
- Setting the speed too high too soon and maintaining poor form throughout the run.

## FAQ

### What treadmill speed equals a 10-minute mile?

Set the treadmill to 6.0 mph for a 10-minute mile pace. For a 9-minute mile, use 6.7 mph. For an 8-minute mile, use 7.5 mph.

### Should I use incline on the treadmill?

A 1-2% incline more accurately simulates the effort of running outdoors, where you don't have a motorized belt assisting your stride. For hill training, increase to 4-8%.

### Is treadmill running easier than outdoor running?

The belt assists leg turnover, making flat treadmill running slightly easier. Setting a 1-2% incline compensates for this difference.

### What muscles does the Treadmill Run (Time) work?

The Treadmill Run (Time) primarily targets the cardiovascular, with secondary activation in the quadriceps, hamstrings, calves, glutes. It's classified as a cardio exercise for the cardio.

### Is the Treadmill Run (Time) good for beginners?

Yes, the Treadmill Run (Time) is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building cardiovascular strength.
