# Kettlebell Double Alternating Hang Clean

## Quick answer

The kettlebell double alternating hang clean is a dynamic exercise that targets the bicep while also engaging the forearms and shoulders. It involves explosive hip and knee extension to lift the kettlebells from a hanging position to shoulder height in an alternating fashion. Intermediate arms exercise using kettlebell. Targets biceps, also works forearms, shoulders.

- **Body Part:** Arms
- **Equipment:** Kettlebell
- **Target Muscle:** Biceps
- **Secondary Muscles:** Forearms, Shoulders
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a kettlebell in each hand with an overhand grip.
2. Bend your knees slightly and hinge forward at the hips, keeping your back straight and chest up.
3. Allow the kettlebells to hang straight down in front of your body.
4. In one fluid motion, explosively extend your hips and knees while shrugging your shoulders.
5. As the kettlebells rise, pull them up towards your shoulders, keeping your elbows high and out to the sides.
6. Catch the kettlebells at shoulder height, with your palms facing inward and your elbows pointing forward.
7. Lower the kettlebells back down to the starting position and repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Drive both kettlebells from the hang position simultaneously using hip extension, but alternate which hand re-cleans on each rep.
- Maintain a strong rack position with elbows tight and kettlebells resting against your forearms between reps.
- Keep your chest up and back flat throughout the hip hinge to protect the lumbar spine under the doubled load.

## Common mistakes

- Pulling with the arms instead of using hip drive, which quickly fatigues the biceps and forearms.
- Letting the kettlebells swing too far forward on the hang, losing the hinge position.
- Not timing the catch properly, resulting in the kettlebells crashing into the forearms.

## FAQ

### What's the difference between a double alternating hang clean and a regular double clean?

In the alternating version, you clean both bells but may re-clean one at a time in an alternating pattern. A standard double clean brings both bells to the rack simultaneously every rep.

### What size kettlebells should I use?

Start with kettlebells 4-8 kg lighter than what you'd use for single-arm cleans. Holding two bells changes the balance and requires more core stabilization.

### What muscles does the Kettlebell Double Alternating Hang Clean work?

The Kettlebell Double Alternating Hang Clean primarily targets the biceps, with secondary activation in the forearms, shoulders. It's classified as a strength exercise for the arms.

### Is the Kettlebell Double Alternating Hang Clean good for beginners?

The Kettlebell Double Alternating Hang Clean is rated as intermediate. Beginners should build a base of biceps strength with easier variations first.
