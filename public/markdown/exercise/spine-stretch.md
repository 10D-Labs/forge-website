# Spine Stretch

## Quick answer

The spine stretch is a bodyweight exercise that focuses on stretching and mobilizing the spine. It also provides a gentle stretch to the hamstrings and glutes. This movement is performed seated and emphasizes spinal flexibility and posture. Beginner back exercise using body weight. Targets lower back, also works hamstrings, glutes.

- **Body Part:** Back
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Lower Back
- **Secondary Muscles:** Hamstrings, Glutes
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Mobility

---

## How to perform

1. Sit on the ground with your legs extended in front of you.
2. Place your hands on the ground behind you, fingers pointing towards your body.
3. Engage your core and slowly lean back, keeping your back straight.
4. Continue leaning back until you feel a stretch in your spine.
5. Hold the stretch for a few seconds, then slowly return to the starting position.
6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Sit on a folded towel or yoga block if your hamstrings are tight, which will help you maintain a tall spine.
- Initiate the stretch by rounding forward from the upper back, vertebra by vertebra, like peeling your spine off an imaginary wall.
- Reach your arms forward as you round to increase the stretch along the entire posterior chain.

## Common mistakes

- Collapsing forward from the hips instead of sequentially rounding through the spine.
- Bouncing at the bottom of the stretch instead of holding a steady, sustained position.
- Locking the knees and pulling on the hamstrings instead of focusing on spinal flexion.

## FAQ

### What does the Spine Stretch target?

It stretches the erector spinae muscles along the entire spine, as well as the hamstrings and the fascia of the posterior chain.

### Is this a Pilates exercise?

Yes, the Spine Stretch forward is a classic Pilates exercise. It emphasizes spinal articulation and flexibility while engaging the deep core muscles.

### How often should I do Spine Stretches?

Daily stretching is ideal for spinal mobility. Hold for 20-30 seconds and repeat 3-5 times. It is especially beneficial after long periods of sitting.

### What muscles does the Spine Stretch work?

The Spine Stretch primarily targets the lower back, with secondary activation in the hamstrings, glutes. It's classified as a mobility exercise for the back.

### Is the Spine Stretch good for beginners?

Yes, the Spine Stretch is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building lower back strength.
