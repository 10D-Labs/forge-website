# Single Arm Isometric Lat Pull - Forge Exercise Library

## Quick Answer

The single arm isometric lat pull is a bodyweight exercise where you place your arm against a wall and attempt to pull downward, creating an isometric contraction in the lat muscles. Since the wall prevents movement, the lat stays contracted under tension.

**Details:**
- **Body Part:** Back
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Lats
- **Secondary Muscles:** Shoulders, Triceps
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to Perform Single Arm Isometric Lat Pull

1. Stand facing a wall with your feet shoulder-width apart.
2. Raise one arm overhead and place your palm flat against the wall.
3. Keeping your arm straight, attempt to pull your hand downward as if doing a lat pulldown.
4. The wall prevents movement, creating an isometric contraction in your lat.
5. Hold the contraction for 10-30 seconds, then release.
6. Repeat with the other arm.
