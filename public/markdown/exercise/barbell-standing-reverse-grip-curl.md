# Barbell Standing Reverse Grip Curl - Forge Exercise Library

## Quick Answer

The barbell standing reverse grip curl is an exercise targeting the bicep and forearms. It is performed by holding a barbell with an underhand grip, curling the weight up while keeping the elbows close to the torso, and then lowering it back down.

**Details:**
- **Body Part:** Arms
- **Equipment:** Barbell
- **Target Muscle:** Biceps
- **Secondary Muscles:** Forearms
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to Perform Barbell Standing Reverse Grip Curl

1. Stand up straight with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a barbell with an underhand grip, palms facing up.
2. Keep your elbows close to your torso and your upper arms stationary.
3. Exhale and curl the weights while contracting your biceps, bringing the barbell as close to your shoulders as possible.
4. Hold the contracted position for a brief pause as you squeeze your biceps.
5. Inhale and slowly lower the barbell back to the starting position.
6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
