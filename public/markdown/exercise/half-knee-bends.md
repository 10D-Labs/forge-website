# Half Knee Bends

## Quick answer

Half knee bends are a bodyweight exercise that primarily targets the cardiovascular system while also engaging the quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes. The movement involves bending the knees and lowering the body as if sitting back into a chair, then returning to standing. This exercise is often used for light cardio, warm-ups, or rehabilitation. Beginner cardio exercise using body weight. Targets cardiovascular, also works quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes.

- **Body Part:** Cardio
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Cardiovascular
- **Secondary Muscles:** Quadriceps, Hamstrings, Glutes
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Cardio

---

## How to perform

1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart.
2. Bend your knees and lower your body down as if you were sitting back into a chair.
3. Keep your chest up and your weight in your heels.
4. Pause for a moment at the bottom, then push through your heels to return to the starting position.
5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Bend your knees only to about 45-60 degrees, keeping the movement in the upper range of a squat.
- Maintain an upright torso and keep your weight evenly distributed across your feet.
- Perform reps at a quick, rhythmic pace to keep your heart rate elevated for cardio benefit.

## Common mistakes

- Bending too deep, which turns it into a full squat and changes it from a cardio exercise to a strength exercise.
- Leaning forward excessively, which puts strain on the lower back.
- Moving too slowly for a meaningful cardiovascular training effect.

## FAQ

### What is the purpose of Half Knee Bends?

They provide a low-impact cardiovascular exercise that also warms up the quads, hamstrings, and glutes. They are excellent for warm-ups or active recovery.

### Are Half Knee Bends good for people with knee problems?

The limited range of motion is generally easier on the knees than full squats. However, anyone with knee pain should start with a very shallow bend and check with a professional if pain occurs.

### How fast should I do Half Knee Bends for cardio?

Aim for 1-2 reps per second to keep your heart rate in the cardio zone. Perform 20-30 reps per set or work for 30-60 second intervals.

### What muscles does the Half Knee Bends work?

The Half Knee Bends primarily targets the cardiovascular, with secondary activation in the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes. It's classified as a cardio exercise for the cardio.

### Is the Half Knee Bends good for beginners?

Yes, the Half Knee Bends is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building cardiovascular strength.
