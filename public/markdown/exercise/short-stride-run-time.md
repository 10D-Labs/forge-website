# Short Stride Run (Time)

## Quick answer

Short stride run is a cardiovascular exercise performed by running with quick, short steps. It primarily targets the cardiovascular system and also engages the quadriceps, hamstrings, and calves. The shorter stride length increases cadence and can help improve running efficiency. Beginner cardio exercise using body weight. Targets cardiovascular, also works quadriceps, hamstrings, calves.

- **Body Part:** Cardio
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Cardiovascular
- **Secondary Muscles:** Quadriceps, Hamstrings, Calves
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Cardio

---

## How to perform

1. Find an open space to perform the exercise.
2. Stand tall with your feet hip-width apart.
3. Start running with quick, short steps, maintaining rapid leg turnover.
4. Keep your feet close to the ground and avoid overstriding.
5. Continue running with short strides for your target duration.

## Pro tips

- Focus on quick cadence (180+ steps per minute) with feet barely leaving the ground.
- Land with your foot directly under your hips, not out in front, to maintain the short stride.
- Keep your shoulders relaxed and arms pumping in short, compact arcs.

## Common mistakes

- Gradually lengthening the stride as fatigue sets in, which defeats the purpose of the drill.
- Tensing the shoulders and neck, which wastes energy and restricts breathing.
- Running on the toes only instead of allowing a quick midfoot-to-toe contact.

## FAQ

### What's the purpose of short stride running?

It trains rapid leg turnover and cadence without the impact stress of full-stride running. It's used to improve running efficiency and is easier on the joints.

### How long should I run with short strides?

Start with 30-60 second intervals with 30 seconds rest. Build up to continuous 5-10 minute efforts at a comfortable pace.

### Is short stride running good for beginners?

Yes. A higher cadence with shorter strides reduces impact forces per step and lowers injury risk, making it one of the safest ways to start a running program.

### What muscles does the Short Stride Run (Time) work?

The Short Stride Run (Time) primarily targets the cardiovascular, with secondary activation in the quadriceps, hamstrings, calves. It's classified as a cardio exercise for the cardio.

### Is the Short Stride Run (Time) good for beginners?

Yes, the Short Stride Run (Time) is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building cardiovascular strength.
