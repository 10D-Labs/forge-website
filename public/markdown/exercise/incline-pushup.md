# Incline Pushup

## Quick answer

The incline pushup is a bodyweight exercise that targets the upper pectoral muscles by elevating the feet, increasing the difficulty compared to a standard pushup. It also engages the tricep and shoulders as secondary muscles. Intermediate chest exercise using body weight. Targets chest, also works triceps, shoulders.

- **Body Part:** Chest
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Chest
- **Secondary Muscles:** Triceps, Shoulders
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Place your hands on the ground slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, with your feet elevated on a stable surface.
2. Keep your body in a straight line from head to toe, engaging your core muscles.
3. Lower your chest towards the ground by bending your elbows, keeping them close to your body.
4. Push through your palms to extend your arms and return to the starting position.
5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Place your feet on a bench or elevated surface with your hands on the floor. The higher your feet, the more this targets the upper chest and front delts.
- Keep your core braced and body in a straight line from head to heels. Do not let your hips sag or pike up.
- Lower your chest all the way to the floor with your elbows at a 45-degree angle from your torso, then press up explosively.

## Common mistakes

- Placing the feet too high before building adequate shoulder and chest strength, which can strain the shoulders.
- Letting the hips sag, which stresses the lower back and reduces chest engagement.
- Flaring the elbows straight out to the sides (90 degrees), which puts excessive stress on the shoulder joints.

## FAQ

### What muscles does the Incline Pushup target?

The feet-elevated Incline Pushup shifts emphasis to the upper chest (clavicular head of the pec) and the anterior deltoids. The higher the feet, the more shoulder-dominant the exercise becomes.

### How high should my feet be for Incline Pushups?

A standard bench (16-18 inches) provides a good balance of upper chest emphasis. A higher surface like a box or wall increases shoulder involvement. Start lower and progress gradually.

### What muscles does the Incline Pushup work?

The Incline Pushup primarily targets the chest, with secondary activation in the triceps, shoulders. It's classified as a strength exercise for the chest.

### Is the Incline Pushup good for beginners?

The Incline Pushup is rated as intermediate. Beginners should build a base of chest strength with easier variations first.
