# Weighted Russian Twist

## Quick answer

The weighted Russian twist is a core exercise that targets the abs and obliques, performed by twisting the torso while holding a weight or medicine ball. Intermediate core exercise using body weight. Targets abs, also works obliques, lower back.

- **Body Part:** Core
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Abs
- **Secondary Muscles:** Obliques, Lower Back
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Sit on the ground with your knees bent and your feet flat on the floor.
2. Hold a weight or medicine ball with both hands in front of your chest.
3. Lean back slightly, keeping your back straight and your core engaged.
4. Slowly twist your torso to the right, bringing the weight or medicine ball towards the floor on your right side.
5. Pause for a moment, then twist your torso to the left, bringing the weight or medicine ball towards the floor on your left side.
6. Continue alternating sides for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Lean back to about a 45-degree angle and maintain that angle throughout to keep constant tension on the abs.
- Touch the weight to the floor on each side to ensure full rotation through the obliques.
- Keep your chest lifted and avoid collapsing your upper back as you twist.

## Common mistakes

- Sitting too upright, which reduces the abdominal demand and makes the exercise too easy.
- Using the arms to swing the weight rather than initiating the twist from the core.
- Holding the breath throughout the set instead of breathing rhythmically with each rotation.

## FAQ

### Do Russian Twists help with love handles?

Russian Twists strengthen the obliques but do not spot-reduce fat. Reducing body fat through diet and overall training is what eliminates love handles.

### What is the best weight to use for Russian Twists?

A medicine ball or dumbbell between 8-20 pounds works for most people. Choose a weight that allows 20-30 total twists with controlled form.

### Should your feet be on the ground or elevated during Russian Twists?

Feet on the ground is easier and better for beginners. Elevating the feet increases difficulty and adds lower ab engagement.

### What muscles does the Weighted Russian Twist work?

The Weighted Russian Twist primarily targets the abs, with secondary activation in the obliques, lower back. It's classified as a strength exercise for the core.

### Is the Weighted Russian Twist good for beginners?

The Weighted Russian Twist is rated as intermediate. Beginners should build a base of abs strength with easier variations first.
