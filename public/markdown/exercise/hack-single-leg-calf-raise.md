# Hack Single Leg Calf Raise

## Quick answer

The hack one leg calf raise is performed on a leg press machine, targeting the calves with additional engagement of the hamstrings and glutes. It is executed one leg at a time, requiring balance and unilateral strength. Intermediate legs exercise using machine. Targets calves, also works hamstrings, glutes.

- **Body Part:** Legs
- **Equipment:** Machine
- **Target Muscle:** Calves
- **Secondary Muscles:** Hamstrings, Glutes
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Adjust the leg press machine to an appropriate weight.
2. Stand on the leg press machine with one foot, keeping the other foot off the ground.
3. Hold onto the handles for stability.
4. Raise your heel as high as possible, lifting your body up on the ball of your foot.
5. Pause for a moment at the top, then slowly lower your heel back down to the starting position.
6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
7. Switch legs and repeat the exercise.

## Pro tips

- Tuck your non-working foot behind your working ankle to keep it out of the way without altering your balance.
- Use about 50-60% of the weight you would use for double-leg Hack Calf Raises to account for the single-leg demand.
- Focus on a slow, full-range movement since single-leg work exposes weaknesses that bilateral training hides.

## Common mistakes

- Using the same weight as the bilateral version, which leads to sloppy form and incomplete range of motion.
- Shifting your hips to one side to compensate, which creates an uneven load on the spine.
- Rushing through reps because the single-leg balance challenge feels uncomfortable.

## FAQ

### Why do single-leg calf raises?

Most people have one calf stronger than the other. Single-leg work identifies and corrects this imbalance, which can reduce injury risk during running and jumping.

### How many reps per leg?

Aim for 10-15 reps per leg with full range of motion. Always train the weaker leg first and match that rep count on the stronger side.

### Is this exercise necessary if I already do bilateral calf raises?

Not strictly necessary, but highly beneficial if you have a noticeable size or strength difference between your calves, or if you play sports with single-leg demands.

### What muscles does the Hack Single Leg Calf Raise work?

The Hack Single Leg Calf Raise primarily targets the calves, with secondary activation in the hamstrings, glutes. It's classified as a strength exercise for the legs.

### Is the Hack Single Leg Calf Raise good for beginners?

The Hack Single Leg Calf Raise is rated as intermediate. Beginners should build a base of calves strength with easier variations first.
