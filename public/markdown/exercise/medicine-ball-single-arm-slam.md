# Medicine Ball Single Arm Slam

## Quick answer

The one arm slam with a medicine ball is a dynamic exercise targeting the abs, shoulders, and back. It involves forcefully slamming a medicine ball to the ground with one arm, requiring core strength, power, and coordination. Intermediate core exercise using medicine ball. Targets abs, also works shoulders, back.

- **Body Part:** Core
- **Equipment:** Medicine Ball
- **Target Muscle:** Abs
- **Secondary Muscles:** Shoulders, Back
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding the medicine ball with one hand in front of your waist.
2. Bend your knees slightly and engage your core.
3. Raise the medicine ball above your head, fully extending your arm.
4. Forcefully slam the medicine ball down to the ground, using your core and shoulders to generate power.
5. Catch the medicine ball on the bounce and repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Generate power from your hips and core, not just your arm; think of throwing the ball through the floor.
- Brace your core before each slam to protect your lower back from the rotational forces.
- Use a dead-bounce (sand-filled) medicine ball to prevent dangerous rebounds toward your face.

## Common mistakes

- Using only the arm to slam the ball, which reduces power output and increases shoulder fatigue.
- Rounding the lower back when picking the ball up off the floor between reps.
- Using a rubber medicine ball that bounces unpredictably, creating a safety hazard.

## FAQ

### What weight medicine ball should I use for single arm slams?

Start with 6-10 lbs. The goal is explosive power, not max weight. You should be able to slam the ball forcefully for all reps without sacrificing speed.

### Are medicine ball slams cardio or strength?

They're a hybrid. Slams build explosive power in the core and shoulders while also driving heart rate up quickly. They work well in both HIIT circuits and power training blocks.

### What type of medicine ball is best for slams?

Use a dead-bounce or slam ball filled with sand. Standard rubber medicine balls bounce back aggressively and can cause injury. Slam balls are designed to absorb impact.

### What muscles does the Medicine Ball Single Arm Slam work?

The Medicine Ball Single Arm Slam primarily targets the abs, with secondary activation in the shoulders, back. It's classified as a strength exercise for the core.

### Is the Medicine Ball Single Arm Slam good for beginners?

The Medicine Ball Single Arm Slam is rated as intermediate. Beginners should build a base of abs strength with easier variations first.
