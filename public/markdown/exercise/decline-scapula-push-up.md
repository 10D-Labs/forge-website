# Decline Scapula Push Up - Forge Exercise Library

## Quick Answer

The decline scapula push up is a bodyweight exercise performed on a bench that primarily targets the serratus anterior. It also engages the tricep and shoulders. The movement emphasizes scapular retraction and protraction, helping to improve shoulder stability and strength.

**Details:**
- **Body Part:** Core
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Serratus
- **Secondary Muscles:** Triceps, Shoulders
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to Perform Decline Scapula Push Up

1. Set up an bench.
2. Place your hands on the bench slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
3. Position your feet on the ground, hip-width apart.
4. Lower your chest towards the bench, keeping your elbows tucked in.
5. As you lower, retract your shoulder blades, squeezing your scapulae together.
6. Push through your palms to extend your arms and return to the starting position.
7. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
