# Cable Wrist Curl - Forge Exercise Library

## Quick Answer

The cable wrist curl is an isolation exercise targeting the forearm muscles, particularly the wrist flexors. It is performed using a cable machine and a straight bar, focusing on curling the wrists upward while keeping the forearms stationary.

**Details:**
- **Body Part:** Arms
- **Equipment:** Cable
- **Target Muscle:** Forearms
- **Secondary Muscles:** Biceps, Brachialis
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to Perform Cable Wrist Curl

1. Attach a straight bar to a low pulley cable machine.
2. Stand facing the machine with your feet shoulder-width apart.
3. Grasp the bar with an underhand grip, palms facing up, and your hands shoulder-width apart.
4. Rest your forearms on a bench or pad, with your wrists hanging off the edge.
5. Keeping your forearms stationary, exhale and curl your wrists upward as far as possible.
6. Pause for a moment at the top, then inhale and slowly lower the bar back to the starting position.
7. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
