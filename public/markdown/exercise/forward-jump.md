# Forward Jump - Forge Exercise Library

## Quick Answer

The forward jump is a plyometric exercise that targets the quadriceps and also works the calves, hamstrings, and glutes. It involves jumping forward explosively from a squat position and landing softly, then repeating the movement.

**Details:**
- **Body Part:** Legs
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Quads
- **Secondary Muscles:** Calves, Hamstrings, Glutes
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Plyometrics

---

## How to Perform Forward Jump

1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart.
2. Bend your knees and lower your body into a squat position.
3. Swing your arms back for momentum.
4. Jump forward explosively, extending your hips, knees, and ankles.
5. Land softly on the balls of your feet and immediately go into the next jump.
6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
