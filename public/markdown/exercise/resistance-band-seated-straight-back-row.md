# Resistance Band Seated Straight Back Row

## Quick answer

The resistance band seated straight back row targets the upper back muscles and is performed seated on the floor with a resistance band looped around the feet. It emphasizes scapular retraction and upper back strength. Beginner back exercise using resistance band. Targets upper back, also works biceps, shoulders.

- **Body Part:** Back
- **Equipment:** Resistance Band
- **Target Muscle:** Upper Back
- **Secondary Muscles:** Biceps, Shoulders
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Sit on the floor with your legs extended and loop the resistance band around your feet.
2. Hold the ends of the resistance band with your hands, palms facing each other.
3. Keep your back straight and lean slightly back, engaging your core.
4. Pull the resistance band towards your chest, squeezing your shoulder blades together.
5. Pause for a moment at the top, then slowly release the tension and return to the starting position.
6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- Sit tall with your legs extended and the band looped around your feet for a consistent anchor.
- Pull the band to your sternum by driving your elbows back and squeezing your shoulder blades together.
- Keep your back straight throughout; do not round forward during the reach or lean back during the pull.

## Common mistakes

- Rounding the back during the forward reach, which puts the lumbar spine in a vulnerable position.
- Pulling with the arms only instead of initiating the row from the shoulder blades.
- Using a band that is too light, which does not provide enough resistance to build upper back strength.

## FAQ

### What muscles does the seated band row target?

It targets the upper back (rhomboids, mid-traps), lats, and biceps, similar to a Cable Seated Row.

### How do I keep good posture during seated band rows?

Think about puffing your chest forward and pulling your shoulders back before you start each rep. Engage your core to maintain a tall, straight spine.

### Can I use a towel instead of a resistance band?

A towel provides no resistance and is only useful for isometric holds. For progressive overload, you need an actual resistance band or cable machine.

### What muscles does the Resistance Band Seated Straight Back Row work?

The Resistance Band Seated Straight Back Row primarily targets the upper back, with secondary activation in the biceps, shoulders. It's classified as a strength exercise for the back.

### Is the Resistance Band Seated Straight Back Row good for beginners?

Yes, the Resistance Band Seated Straight Back Row is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building upper back strength.
