# Weighted Front Plank

## Quick answer

The weighted front plank is a core stability exercise that increases the challenge of the traditional plank by adding external weight, requiring greater strength and endurance from the abdominal muscles, shoulders, and lower back. Intermediate core exercise using weight plate. Targets abs, also works shoulders, lower back.

- **Body Part:** Core
- **Equipment:** Weight Plate
- **Target Muscle:** Abs
- **Secondary Muscles:** Shoulders, Lower Back
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Start by lying face down on the floor.
2. Place your forearms on the ground, with your elbows directly under your shoulders.
3. Have a partner place a weight plate on your upper back, or position it yourself before getting into position.
4. Extend your legs straight out behind you, with your toes on the ground.
5. Engage your core and lift your body off the ground, balancing on your forearms and toes.
6. Keep your body in a straight line from your head to your heels.
7. Hold this position for your target duration.

## Pro tips

- Position the weight plate on your upper back between the shoulder blades, not on the lower back where it can cause spinal compression.
- Actively push the floor away with your forearms and tuck your pelvis slightly to maximize ab engagement.
- Start with a 10-25 lb plate and master holding for 30 seconds before adding more weight.

## Common mistakes

- Placing the weight plate too low on the back, which increases lumbar stress and can cause lower back pain.
- Letting the hips sag under the added load, which transfers stress to the spine instead of the abs.
- Holding your breath, which spikes blood pressure. Breathe steadily through the hold.

## FAQ

### How much weight should I use for a weighted plank?

Start with a 10-25 lb plate. If you can hold a bodyweight plank for over 60 seconds with good form, you are ready to add weight.

### Is a weighted plank better than a longer bodyweight plank?

Yes, once you can hold a regular plank for 60+ seconds, adding weight is more effective for building core strength than simply holding longer.

### Where do I place the weight plate for a weighted plank?

Place it on your upper back between the shoulder blades. Having a training partner place it is the safest approach.

### What muscles does the Weighted Front Plank work?

The Weighted Front Plank primarily targets the abs, with secondary activation in the shoulders, lower back. It's classified as a strength exercise for the core.

### Is the Weighted Front Plank good for beginners?

The Weighted Front Plank is rated as intermediate. Beginners should build a base of abs strength with easier variations first.
