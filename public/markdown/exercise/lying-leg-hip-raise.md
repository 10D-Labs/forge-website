# Lying Leg Hip Raise

## Quick answer

The lying leg hip raise is an abdominal exercise performed on the floor using only body weight. It targets the abs and also works the hip flexors. The movement involves lifting the legs and hips off the ground while keeping the core engaged, then lowering them back down with control. Beginner core exercise using body weight. Targets abs, also works hip flexors.

- **Body Part:** Core
- **Equipment:** Body Weight
- **Target Muscle:** Abs
- **Secondary Muscles:** Hip Flexors
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Lie flat on your back with your legs extended and your arms by your sides.
2. Place your hands under your glutes for support.
3. Engage your core and lift your legs off the ground, raising them towards the ceiling.
4. Pause for a moment at the top, then slowly lower your legs back down to the starting position.
5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

## Pro tips

- At the top of the movement, push your feet straight toward the ceiling to lift your hips off the ground for maximum lower ab activation.
- Keep your legs as straight as possible and avoid bending the knees as you raise them.
- Press your hands firmly into the ground beside you to stabilize your torso.

## Common mistakes

- Using momentum to swing the legs up instead of lifting them with controlled abdominal contraction.
- Arching the lower back off the ground at the bottom, which strains the lumbar spine.
- Skipping the hip raise portion and only doing a leg raise, which misses the lower ab peak contraction.

## FAQ

### What is the difference between a leg raise and a leg hip raise?

A standard leg raise stops when the legs are vertical. The leg hip raise adds a pelvic curl at the top, lifting the hips off the ground, which increases lower ab activation.

### Can you do this exercise if you have lower back pain?

Only if you can keep your lower back pressed flat against the floor throughout. If your back arches, bend your knees or reduce the range of motion.

### How many reps of Lying Leg Hip Raises should you do?

Aim for 3 sets of 10-15 reps with controlled tempo. Quality of each rep matters more than quantity.

### What muscles does the Lying Leg Hip Raise work?

The Lying Leg Hip Raise primarily targets the abs, with secondary activation in the hip flexors. It's classified as a strength exercise for the core.

### Is the Lying Leg Hip Raise good for beginners?

Yes, the Lying Leg Hip Raise is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building abs strength.
