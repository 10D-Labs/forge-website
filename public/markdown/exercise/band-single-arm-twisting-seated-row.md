# Band Single Arm Twisting Seated Row

## Quick answer

The band one arm twisting seated row is a resistance exercise targeting the upper back, with secondary emphasis on the bicep and shoulders. It incorporates a twisting motion to engage the core and improve rotational strength. Intermediate back exercise using resistance band. Targets upper back, also works biceps, shoulders.

- **Body Part:** Back
- **Equipment:** Resistance Band
- **Target Muscle:** Upper Back
- **Secondary Muscles:** Biceps, Shoulders
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Sit on a bench or chair with your feet flat on the ground and your back straight.
2. Hold the band with one hand and extend your arm fully in front of you.
3. Keeping your back straight, pull the band towards your body by bending your elbow and squeezing your shoulder blades together.
4. At the same time, twist your torso towards the side of the pulling arm.
5. Pause for a moment at the top, then slowly release the tension in the band and return to the starting position.
6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, then switch to the other arm.

## Pro tips

- Sit tall and initiate the pull by retracting the shoulder blade, then add a torso twist toward the pulling arm.
- Keep your feet firmly planted and avoid letting the band pull you forward.
- Twist only through the thoracic spine while keeping your lower back stable.

## Common mistakes

- Rounding the upper back forward during the release phase, losing the tall seated posture.
- Twisting too aggressively, which causes the lower back to rotate instead of isolating thoracic rotation.
- Using the bicep to pull without first engaging the back by retracting the shoulder blade.

## FAQ

### What does adding a twist to a Seated Row accomplish?

The twist engages the obliques and thoracic rotators while the row trains the upper back. It mimics real-world pulling patterns that involve rotation.

### Should you twist toward or away from the pulling arm?

Twist toward the pulling arm. As you row your right hand toward you, your torso rotates to the right, allowing a greater range of motion for the back muscles.

### Can this replace a Cable Seated Row?

It can for home workouts, but cable rows allow heavier loading. The band version is better for functional training and rehab purposes.

### What muscles does the Band Single Arm Twisting Seated Row work?

The Band Single Arm Twisting Seated Row primarily targets the upper back, with secondary activation in the biceps, shoulders. It's classified as a strength exercise for the back.

### Is the Band Single Arm Twisting Seated Row good for beginners?

The Band Single Arm Twisting Seated Row is rated as intermediate. Beginners should build a base of upper back strength with easier variations first.
