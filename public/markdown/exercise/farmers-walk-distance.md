# Farmers Walk (Distance)

## Quick answer

The farmer's walk is a functional strength exercise where you walk while holding a dumbbell in each hand, engaging the quads, calves, forearms, and core. It improves grip strength, core stability, and overall conditioning. Beginner legs exercise using dumbbell. Targets quads, also works calves, forearms, core.

- **Body Part:** Legs
- **Equipment:** Dumbbell
- **Target Muscle:** Quads
- **Secondary Muscles:** Calves, Forearms, Core
- **Difficulty:** Beginner
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Stand up straight with a dumbbell in each hand, palms facing your sides.
2. Keep your back straight and your shoulders back.
3. Take small, controlled steps forward, maintaining an upright posture.
4. Continue walking until you reach your target distance.
5. To finish, stop walking and carefully lower the dumbbells to your sides.

## Pro tips

- Choose a weight that's challenging but allows you to maintain perfect upright posture for the entire distance.
- Take short, controlled steps and avoid swaying from side to side.
- Grip the dumbbells as tightly as possible to build forearm and grip strength.

## Common mistakes

- Using weights that are too heavy, causing the torso to lean forward and the shoulders to round.
- Taking long strides, which creates lateral sway and destabilizes the core.
- Letting the dumbbells rest against the legs, which reduces the stabilization demand.

## FAQ

### How heavy should the dumbbells be for farmer's walks?

A good starting point is 50% of your body weight per hand. So a 160 lb person would use two 80 lb dumbbells. Adjust based on your grip strength and the distance.

### What muscles do farmer's walks work?

Nearly everything: grip, forearms, traps, shoulders, core, quads, glutes, and calves. It's one of the most complete full-body exercises available.

### How far should I walk?

40-60 yards per set is standard. For heavy loads, 20-30 yards is sufficient. For lighter loads and conditioning, extend to 80-100 yards.

### What muscles does the Farmers Walk (Distance) work?

The Farmers Walk (Distance) primarily targets the quads, with secondary activation in the calves, forearms, core. It's classified as a strength exercise for the legs.

### Is the Farmers Walk (Distance) good for beginners?

Yes, the Farmers Walk (Distance) is rated as a beginner-level exercise. It's a great starting point for building quads strength.
