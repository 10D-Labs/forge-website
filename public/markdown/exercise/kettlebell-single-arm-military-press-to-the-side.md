# Kettlebell Single Arm Military Press to the Side

## Quick answer

The kettlebell one arm military press to the side is a unilateral overhead pressing movement that targets the deltoids, with secondary emphasis on the tricep and core for stabilization. It requires balance, coordination, and shoulder strength. Intermediate shoulders exercise using kettlebell. Targets shoulders, also works triceps, core.

- **Body Part:** Shoulders
- **Equipment:** Kettlebell
- **Target Muscle:** Shoulders
- **Secondary Muscles:** Triceps, Core
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Category:** Strength

---

## How to perform

1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a kettlebell in one hand at shoulder height.
2. Engage your core and keep your back straight.
3. Press the kettlebell overhead, extending your arm fully.
4. Pause for a moment at the top, then slowly lower the kettlebell back to the starting position.
5. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions, then switch sides.

## Pro tips

- Press the kettlebell slightly out to the side rather than straight overhead, which targets the lateral deltoid more than a standard Military Press.
- Keep your elbow under the kettlebell throughout the press to maintain a strong base of support.
- Brace your core hard and squeeze the opposite fist to create full-body tension (irradiation principle).

## Common mistakes

- Leaning away from the pressing side to compensate for the weight, which strains the spine.
- Pressing the bell too far to the side, turning it into a Lateral Raise pattern that stresses the shoulder.
- Not locking out fully overhead before lowering.

## FAQ

### Why press to the side instead of straight up?

The slight lateral angle increases demand on the lateral deltoid and obliques compared to a standard Military Press. It's a variation that hits the shoulders from a different angle.

### How heavy should I go?

Use about 70-80% of your regular kettlebell Military Press weight. The lateral angle reduces your mechanical advantage.

### What muscles does the Kettlebell Single Arm Military Press to the Side work?

The Kettlebell Single Arm Military Press to the Side primarily targets the shoulders, with secondary activation in the triceps, core. It's classified as a strength exercise for the shoulders.

### Is the Kettlebell Single Arm Military Press to the Side good for beginners?

The Kettlebell Single Arm Military Press to the Side is rated as intermediate. Beginners should build a base of shoulders strength with easier variations first.
